Exploring the Seas with Ginori 1735 and a Hint of Mystery ...

Luxe by Design: The Ginori Terrace Experience • Savor the Moments: Dining with Italian Flair • The Craftsmanship Behind <em>Il Viaggio di Nettuno</em> • A Symphony of Colors and Narratives • Unveiling the Ginori Touch: A Step Beyond Luxury • More ...

By Renee  · May 16, 2026

Have you ever dreamed of escaping to a world where time slows to the rhythm of the sea, the horizon stretches endlessly into the sky, and luxury feels both intimate and effortless? Step aboard the magnificent Evrima from The Ritz-Carlton Yacht Collection, where the breathtaking Ginori Terrace transforms ocean travel into an immersive celebration of Italian artistry, storytelling, and refined leisure. Designed in collaboration with GINORI 1735, one of Italy’s oldest porcelain houses founded in 1735 near Florence, this extraordinary open-air retreat blends maritime elegance with centuries of craftsmanship.

The terrace draws inspiration from Il Viaggio di Nettuno (“The Voyage of Neptune”), the imaginative porcelain collection created by British artist and designer Luke Edward Hall for Ginori 1735. Known for his romantic reinterpretations of classical mythology and Mediterranean culture, Hall infused the collection with sea gods, mythical creatures, striped motifs, and playful decorative storytelling. Every detail aboard Evrima reflects this visual narrative, weaving together mythology, travel, and Italian design culture into one cohesive sensory experience.

The Ginori Terrace unfolds across three open-air atmospheres, each capturing a different mood of life at sea. Imagine quiet mornings beside a reflective pool, sun-drenched afternoons beneath striped parasols inspired by classic Riviera beach clubs, and golden evenings illuminated by sunset hues dancing across porcelain surfaces and polished teak decks. It is more than a luxury cruise experience — it is a living expression of contemporary grand touring at sea, elevated through art and design.

Luxe by Design: The Ginori Terrace Experience

Photo: @ginori1735

The first step onto the Ginori Terrace feels cinematic. Lounge chairs wrapped in crisp nautical blue-and-white stripes mirror the movement of the waves beyond, creating a seamless visual dialogue between yacht and ocean. The design language intentionally references the timeless glamour of the Italian Riviera and Mediterranean coastal resorts while maintaining the understated sophistication associated with The Ritz-Carlton brand.

At the center, the shimmering pool reflects the changing sky throughout the day, becoming both a design focal point and a tranquil sanctuary from the midday sun. Unlike traditional mega-cruise ships that emphasize scale and spectacle, Evrima was conceived as an intimate ultra-luxury yacht experience, accommodating approximately 298 guests in spacious suite-style accommodations with private terraces. This boutique scale allows spaces like the Ginori Terrace to feel curated rather than crowded, immersive rather than overwhelming.

Beyond aesthetics, the terrace embodies a lifestyle philosophy where craftsmanship, leisure, and storytelling coexist in harmony. Through Il Viaggio di Nettuno, Ginori 1735 extends its artistic vision beyond porcelain into textiles, furnishings, and spatial design, transforming the terrace into a cohesive environment where every detail contributes to the atmosphere of refined exploration.

Savor the Moments: Dining with Italian Flair

Photo: @ginori1735

Dining aboard Evrima becomes an artistic ritual in itself. Tables adorned with Ginori 1735 porcelain feature hand-illustrated mythological motifs, marine-inspired detailing, and rich Mediterranean tones that elevate every course into an occasion. Whether enjoying a leisurely breakfast beneath open skies or lingering over aperitivo hour while the sea stretches endlessly in the distance, each moment feels carefully choreographed yet effortlessly relaxed.

The collaboration reflects a broader movement in luxury hospitality where dining extends beyond cuisine into multisensory storytelling. Ginori’s porcelain, long associated with aristocratic European tables and prestigious residences, brings historical depth to the contemporary yachting experience. Since the 18th century, the Florentine house has produced pieces admired for their fusion of artistry and technical precision, becoming synonymous with Italian decorative excellence.

There is also a distinctly theatrical quality to the terrace dining experience. The interplay between sunlight, porcelain glazes, striped linens, and ocean reflections creates constantly shifting compositions throughout the day. With every course served, a dialogue unfolds between culinary craftsmanship and ceramic artistry — between mythology and modernity, tradition and reinvention.

The Craftsmanship Behind Il Viaggio di Nettuno

Photo: @ginori1735

Designed by Luke Edward Hall, Il Viaggio di Nettuno celebrates the mythology of Neptune, the Roman god of the sea. The collection features whimsical depictions of sea creatures, classical figures, decorative borders, and Mediterranean-inspired ornamentation rendered through Hall’s unmistakably expressive style. The result is a collection that feels simultaneously ancient and contemporary — rooted in mythology yet unmistakably modern in execution.

GINORI 1735 has long been celebrated for preserving traditional Italian porcelain-making techniques while embracing contemporary artistic collaborations. Each porcelain piece reflects centuries of Florentine craftsmanship, balancing technical mastery with imaginative storytelling. This dedication to artisanal heritage has allowed the brand to remain relevant across generations while maintaining its reputation as one of Europe’s most respected luxury porcelain manufacturers.

The partnership with The Ritz-Carlton Yacht Collection also reflects a growing convergence between luxury hospitality and collectible design. Rather than merely decorating a space, Ginori 1735 created a complete visual identity that transforms Evrima into a floating extension of Italian artistic culture.

A Symphony of Colors and Narratives

Photo: @ginori1735

The Ginori Terrace is a masterclass in color storytelling. Soft blush pinks transition into nautical blues, while warm neutrals and sunlit whites mirror the changing atmosphere of the sea itself. Throughout the day, the environment evolves naturally with shifting light — from delicate pastel mornings to vivid sapphire afternoons and amber-toned evenings.

The striped umbrellas and textiles subtly reference the glamour of vintage European seaside resorts, yet the overall composition remains fresh and contemporary rather than nostalgic. Each section of the terrace offers a distinct emotional energy: peaceful solitude beneath shaded loungers, lively social moments over cocktails, or reflective quiet during sunset hours.

This layered visual identity mirrors the unpredictability and romance of maritime travel itself. Just as the sea continuously changes mood and texture, the terrace transforms alongside it, creating a deeply immersive atmosphere where design feels alive rather than static.

Unveiling the Ginori Touch: A Step Beyond Luxury

Photo: @ginori1735

What makes the Ginori Terrace aboard Evrima so remarkable is not merely its visual beauty, but the philosophy it represents. It embodies a modern interpretation of luxury that values artistry, heritage, intimacy, and emotional experience over excess alone.

Through this collaboration, GINORI 1735 extends its legacy beyond traditional tableware into experiential design, proving how craftsmanship can shape not only objects but entire environments. Meanwhile, The Ritz-Carlton Yacht Collection continues redefining luxury travel by combining boutique hospitality with the freedom and romance of yachting.

The result is a destination that feels suspended between worlds — part floating hotel, part art installation, part Mediterranean dreamscape. Every porcelain detail, textile pattern, and architectural composition contributes to a journey that celebrates not only travel itself, but the enduring beauty of Italian craftsmanship and storytelling.

Have you ever experienced a form of travel where design, history, art, and atmosphere blended together so seamlessly? The Ginori Terrace aboard Evrima invites you to imagine a slower, more beautiful way of exploring the world — one shaped by craftsmanship, culture, and the timeless romance of the sea.
