41 Sights of San Francisco, the City on the Bay ...

Bay Bridge • Golden Gate Bridge • Golden Gate Park • Huntington Falls • The Japanese Tea Garden • More ...

By Neecey

Some of the sights of San Francisco are iconic and known around the world, others are less familiar but still help make it a great city. San Francisco is a brilliant place to visit and has a vibe all of its own. Here are some of the sights of San Francisco to put on a visitor's itinerary.

1. Bay Bridge

Via Happy New Year!
Everyone knows the Golden Gate Bridge but the Bay Bridge is one of the great sights of San Francisco.

2. Golden Gate Bridge

Via Believe in Yourself.
One of the most well known bridges in the world. It must have been blown up in the movies so many times.

3. Golden Gate Park

Via Golden Gate Park, San Francisco, ...
The park is huge (20% bigger than Central Park) covering more than 1,000 acres. It has so many features you can easily spend a whole week in the park alone. Some pics of a few of the features follow.

4. Huntington Falls

Via wildnatureimages.com
The falls cascade down Strawberry Hill into Stow Lake.

5. The Japanese Tea Garden

Via 30 | November | 2011 ...
This is the oldest Japanese garden in the US and was designed in 1894.

6. Koret Children's Quarter

Via How to spend a day ...
Fun things for the kids to do.

7. Flower Conservatory

Via flower observatory
One of the world's largest conservatories, it's built of traditional wood and glass panes

8. Golden Gate Park Lakes

Via Heaven&#x27;s Gate
There are nine lakes in Golden Gate Park and they have boating and model boat facilities.

9. Golden Gate Park Windmill

Via 20 Things to See in ...
The North Windmill is one of two in the park.

10. Palace of Fine Arts

One of the most photogenic attractions of San Francisco.

11. California Academy of Sciences

Via California Academy of Sciences / ...
One of the largest natural history museums in the world, the building covers 37,000 square meters of Golden Gate Park.

Time to leave the park now and see some other sights of San Francisco.

12. Mosaic Steps

Via inthralld.com
You'll find these on 16th Avenue.

13. Trolley Cars

Via The Famous San Francisco Cable ...
Every visitor to San Fran should go on at least one trolley bus ride, for the experience and the views.

14. Land's End Labyrinth

Via lands end labyrinth
Land's End is a park in the Golden Gate National Recreation Area.

15. Alcatraz

Via Fabulous places to visit or ...
Catch a ferry at Pier 33 to take the tour.

16. The Green Columbus Building

The building is the headquarters of Zoetrope, Francis Ford Coppola's film company, and a darling cafe on the ground floor called Zoetrope.

17. Lombard Street

Via Top 10 attractions to visit ...
Eight steep hairpin bends have to be driven at 5mph.

18. Ferry Plaza Marketplace

Via The San Francisco Ferry Building
Part of the ferry terminal building is now given over to a farmers' market which is a bit of a foodie paradise.

19. Fishermen's Wharf

Via Fishermen's Wharf, San Francisco at ...
Although it's a working wharf, it is one of the great attractions of San Francisco.

20. Ocean Beach

Via Ocean Beach
Very popular with surfers.

21. Seward Street Slides

Via Seward Street Slides
No need to go all the way to the park when the slides are right on the block.

22. The Port

Via Sunrise Over the Port of ...
San Francisco Bay is one of the world's three greatest natural harbors along with Sydney, Australia and Rio de Janeiro, Brazil,

23. Pier 7 Lantern Dock

Via polopixel.com
One of the best places for a spot of fishing.

24. Palace Hotel

Via Palace Hotel, San Francisco
The historic hotel, built in 1909, stands directly on the site where the Old Place Hotel stood before it was razed to the ground in the Great Fire of 1906.

25. The Transamerica Pyramid

Via The Transamerica Pyramid - San ...
San Francisco's tallest building.

26. The Walt Disney Family Museum

Via The Walt Disney Family Museum
Standing in the Presidio with the back presenting a stunning view across the bay, the museum is a must visit for every Disney fan.

27. North Beach

Via North Beach at dusk
The church in the foreground is the Cathedral of Saint Peter and Paul.

28. The Dandelion Chocolate Factory

Via 10 Free and Cheap Things ...
One of the fun free things to do in San Francisco is a tour of the chocolate factory.

29. The De Young Museum

Via 10 Free and Cheap Things ...
Get stunning views from the observation floor.

30. The Streets of San Francisco

Via Men's Fashion, Lifestyle & Photography ...
Isn't this one of the views of San Francisco that always comes to mind?

31. San Francisco

Via San Francisco by Anne-Solange Tardy
Whereas this is somewhat unexpected.

32. Street Cafe

Via san francisco on film: outer ...
The California weather means there's a vibrant street life and cafe culture.

33. Union Square

Via Union Square San Francisco
A California Historical Landmark and a popular place with residents and visitors.

34. Chinatown

Via San Francisco &#8211; Little big ...
San Fran's Chinatown is the oldest and longest continuous running Chinatown in the Western Hemisphere

35. Coit Tower

Via Coit Tower
The art deco tower serves no purpose other than to beautify the city. Coit Tower was paid for with money left by Lillie Hitchcock Coit, a wealthy socialite

36. Telegraph Hill

Via Top 10 highlights of San ...
Coit tower stands at the top of Telegraph Hill, one of the original Seven Hills of San Francisco. Did you know there are 44 hills in San Francisco?

37. Aquarium of the Bay

Via Aquarium of the Bay #66
The best place to go see the local marine life of the Bay area up close and personal.

38. Pier 39

Via San Francisco - Pier 39 ...
The Aquarium stands at the edge of Pier 39 which is home to plenty of fun entertainment and also the Marine Mammal Center.

39. Haas-Lilienthal House

Via National Register #73000438: Haas-Lilienthal House ...
One of the wonderful Victorian houses that managed to survive the Great Earthquake and the Great Fire.

40. Painted Ladies

Via Is San Francisco's Housing Market ...
A cluster of Victorian houses at 710-720 Steiner Street.

41. The City

Via camerondavidson.com
By day or night, the sights of San Francisco are epic.

We've come to the end of our little photo album of the sights and attractions of San Francisco. I hope you enjoyed it. Have you been? Residents of San Fran, please share your favorite places with us.
