# 7 of the Best Free Things to do in Dallas ...

Oh boy – there’s a huge list of free things to do in Dallas. There are simply so many reasons to visit Dallas but knowing that you can have a whale of a time on a dime in the fabulous Texan city has to be among the best. Book a cheap flight, take the train or ride the Greyhound for some massive fun with the free things to do in Dallas.

## 1. Mary Kay Cosmetics

A visit to the Mary Kay Cosmetics headquarters will give you a chance to walk through history and see how Mary Kay Ash managed to build a company that helped so many women find a life outside their kitchens. They have a free museum in their headquarters where you will come face to face with some impressive pictures of Mary Kay as well as some prestigious awards she received over the years – other things include magazines featuring the company, seminar gowns, product evolution and packaging, career apparel and much more. A visit to this place is one of the best free things to do in Dallas and you should arrange this trip whether you've ever been associated with this pink company or not – you will definitely revel in the glory of this special place.

## 2. Free Admission Museums

You no longer need money to get cultured, as admission to some of the best museums in Dallas is completely free of cost. Start from the Dallas Museum of Art where there's over 22,000 works of art under one roof. Once you've absorbed artwork from all time periods and media, take a walk to the adjacent Klyde Park. Other popular museums are the Crow Collection of Asian Art, the Museum of Geometric and MADI Art, and the Old Red Museum of Dallas County History and Culture where you can find thousands of exhibits with historical artifacts, and four mini-theaters. A visit to African American Museum will also prove extremely revealing, as this one-of-a-kind museum puts African-American cultural, artistic, and historical materials on display.

## 3. The Adrian E. Flatt, M.D. Hand Collection at Baylor

A trip here is one of the most interesting and quirkiest free things to do in Dallas. Adrian E.Flatt was a hand surgeon who built a collection of bronze-coated hands that penned famous books, ruled the nation, hit World Series homers, and accepted Academy Awards. You won't have to pay a penny to be able to view this collection and notice the contrast of sizes in more than 100 cast, bronze-coated hands which includes people like Walter Disney, Katherine Hepburn, and Louis Armstrong

## 4. M-Line Trolley

Ding! Ding! Ding! It's time to take a free ride on an M-Line trolley, which is probably one of the best ways to get around Uptown. These trolleys stop at a number of points of interest, such as Hotel Zaza, West Village, Dallas Museum of Art, shops at the Crescent, and more. Try this folks; there isn't a more charming and relaxing way to see Uptown as well as the edge of Downtown Dallas.

## 5. White Rock Lake

Located right in the middle of central Dallas, White Rock Lake is a manmade water and park area and is definitely one of the very best free attractions in Dallas. Built in 1911, the area is now a popular destination for tourists and locals alike. With over 1,000 acres of wooded parkland and more than 9.3 miles of biking trails and jogging tracks, you'll definitely have a great time here. For those who love water sports, this is a perfect place for paddle boarding, rowing, canoeing, and sailing – fishing is also one of favored pastimes here. To have an even better experience, come in January when the March of Dimes Walk America takes place in this park area.

## 6. Neighborhood Sightseeing

No trip to Dallas is complete without exploring a few neighborhoods and communities. Go off to Turtle Creek and Highland Park to see greenscapes and luxury homes, or visit the country in the Munger Place Historic District to marvel at the large collection of prairie-style homes. Stick around for the vibrant nightlife of Lower Greenville Avenue where cafés, bars, and live music venues are usually jam-packed on the weekends.

## 7. Public Art Walk

You cannot keep this self-guided tour off the list of free things to do in Dallas. You'll be on a 3.3-mile route highlighting around 30 pieces of art and architecture – you will get to see contemporary sculptures, classic architecture, Dallas icons, and so much more.

With so many brilliant free things to do in Dallas there’s no excuse not to visit. Ready to book a trip?
