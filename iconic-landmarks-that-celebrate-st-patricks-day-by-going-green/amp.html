35 Iconic Landmarks Taking Part in Global Greening to Celebrate St. Patricks Day 2019 ...

By Neecey

St. Patrick’s Day is one of the few national holidays that is celebrated internationally. It seems everyone wants to claim even a tiny bit of Irish heritage on March 17th (my paternal grandfather was Irish – born in Belfast) and why not? St. Patrick’s Day celebrations are amazing and all-embracing and welcoming. But it’s not all about drinking green beer, parades, and listening to Irish music. Thanks to Tourism Ireland, there will be 300+ landmarks which include stadiums, statues, museums and towers, around the world will be going green for St. Patrick’s Day in 2019. Frequently Asked Questions

What is Global Greening for St. Patrick's Day?
Global Greening is a tradition where famous landmarks worldwide are illuminated in green to celebrate St. Patrick's Day.
Why do landmarks light up green on St. Patrick's Day?
Lighting landmarks green honors Irish culture and raises awareness for Irish heritage around the world.
Can I visit these landmarks on St. Patrick's Day?
Many landmarks are open to visitors year-round, and visiting on St. Patrick's Day offers a special festive experience.
Which famous landmarks participate in Global Greening?
Iconic spots like the Sydney Opera House, the Eiffel Tower, and the Colosseum have all taken part in Global Greening.
Is Global Greening only celebrated in Ireland?
No, landmarks across many countries participate to spread Irish culture globally.
How can I see pictures of landmarks during Global Greening?
You can find photos on tourism websites and social media using hashtags like #GlobalGreening.
Are there special events at these landmarks during St. Patrick's Day?
Some landmarks host events or light shows to enhance the celebration; check local listings for details.
How can I participate in St. Patrick's Day celebrations if I can't visit a landmark?
You can celebrate by wearing green, joining local parades, or hosting a themed gathering with friends.
Does Global Greening support any causes?
Yes, it often supports Irish tourism and cultural initiatives worldwide.
When did the Global Greening tradition start?
It began in 2010 as an initiative to promote Irish culture globally through iconic landmarks.
