# 8 Super Things to do in Singapore ...

There are some wonderful things to do in Singapore. It is a country which has thrown off the shackles of its colonial history, and is one of the major players in world tourism. It’s one of the few Asian countries that has developed the taste of the west, whilst at the same time maintaining its oriental history, meaning there’s a great mix of the ancient and modern in the things to do in Singapore. Go and see for yourself what this marvelous little country has to offer - you can be sure there will be mass amounts of things to do in Singapore.

## 1. River Cruise

Hop on a bumboat and cruise down the Singapore River. Witness the colonial buildings and old shops where so many people attempted to make a living on this once-chaotic stretch of river serenity. Of all the things to do in Singapore, this is the activity to get deep into the underbelly of this country.

## 2. Chili Crabs

The masterfully prepared Chili Crab is a delicacy not to be missed. This is one of the things to eat in Singapore if you want an authentic taste of the local cuisine. Essentially, it’s fried crab draped in special gravy. Sweet, spicy, and savory are three mouth-watering descriptions of this essential Singapore signature dish.

## 3. National Museum of Singapore

There are so many places to visit in Singapore, and to see the country’s history and culture, go to the National Museum of Singapore. Witness the rich heritage of the country as it struggled to make its way in a world of violence and conflict, before it was taken over and enslaved by the British Empire, and finally onto independence and its own unique place in the world. History buffs will love spending some time here!

## 4. Wet Markets

Wet markets are a unique activity you won’t find in the West. Simply put, it’s a supermarket in an open-air setting. The name comes from Singapore’s stifling humidity where everything is wet from the moisture in the air. It’s the freshest produce you are ever going to find - this is ‘straight from the source’ food.

## 5. Singapore Sling

Any list of things to do in Singapore should involve sampling the food and drink. If you’ve already munched your way through heaving bowls of chili crab, it’s time to move on to sip a Singapore Sling. This delicious cocktail was invented in the famed Raffles Hotel at the turn of the 20th century. Take a seat on the hotel verandah, call over the waiter and watch the sunset while you enjoy the concoction of gin, Cherry Heering, Bénédictine, and fresh pineapple juice.

## 6. Sentosa Island

Sentosa Island is one of the few things to see in Singapore which absolutely can’t be passed up on. It contains a permanent exhibition known as the ‘Images of Singapore’. Locals and tourists alike set aside some time to come here and look at the colorful art painted by Singaporean artists. Folk lore and tales from the old times are presented in this artistic fashion to better explain the nature of this fairly unique land.

## 7. Fly through Singapore

The Singapore Flyer is where you get to ride an observation wheel and reach 165 meters above the ground when at the top. Spectacular views over Marina Bay Sands, Sentosa Island, and the Singapore River are afforded from this height. It truly is the most iconic way to see the sights.

## 8. Hawker Food

Most of the things to eat in Singapore can be found in the form of Hawker Food. Situated within Hawker Centers plastered all throughout the country, these fairs provide people with the chance to turn hunger into righteous gluttony with the authentic Singaporean dishes of Chicken Rice, Laksa, Rojak, and Fried Hokkien Prawn.

Work your way through this list of things to see in Singapore and you can experience a vacation like no other. Things to eat in Singapore can open your taste buds to something entirely new, whilst the selection of things to do in Singapore will provide you with a new perspective on this former British colony.
