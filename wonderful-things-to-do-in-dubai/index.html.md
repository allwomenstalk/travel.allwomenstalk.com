# 8 Wonderful Things to do in Dubai ...

**Things to do in Dubai are going to keep you well occupied**. This shimmering desert metropolis is no ordinary destination - it’s a place where imagination meets ambition, and the result is nothing short of extraordinary. Rising from the golden sands of the Arabian Desert, Dubai is the brainchild of visionary leaders who dared to dream beyond the limits of geography and tradition. What was once a modest fishing village has evolved into **one of the most dynamic and forward-thinking cities in the world**, seamlessly blending ultramodern innovation with the rich cultural heritage of the Emirates.

**To experience Dubai** is to immerse yourself in a city that celebrates extremes: the tallest building, the largest mall, the most luxurious hotel, the most ambitious man-made islands. But beyond the glittering façade of superlatives lies a destination that also knows how to surprise you with its subtleties - from serene desert landscapes and traditional souks to world-class **art spaces and Michelin-starred dining**.

**Dubai’s offerings span every interest and curiosity**, making it a destination perfectly suited to thoughtfully curated [Dubai Travel Packages](https://www.memphistours.com/dubai/dubai-travel-packages) designed around every traveler’s passion. Whether you’re an architecture enthusiast eager to marvel at the iconic Burj Khalifa, a thrill-seeker chasing the desert dunes, a shopaholic exploring the **opulence of Dubai Mall**, or a culture lover searching for authenticity in the **Al Fahidi Historical District**, there is no shortage of extraordinary experiences waiting to unfold.

One of Dubai’s most remarkable traits is its **ability to constantly reinvent itself**. Each year brings new landmarks, attractions, and cultural experiences that challenge perceptions of what a city can be. Yet amid the rapid development, it manages to preserve elements of tradition, allowing visitors to engage with the UAE’s rich Bedouin roots and Islamic heritage through **museums, mosques, and immersive tours**.

From sun-drenched beaches and luxury yachts to futuristic gardens and high-end gastronomy, **Dubai invites you to explore on your own terms**, at your own pace-be it indulgent, adventurous, or contemplative. The scale is grand, the possibilities endless.

So, if you’re wondering where to start, let this be your guide. Here is my **curated list of 8 Wonderful Things to Do in Dubai**, designed to help you navigate this incredible city and make the most of your time in one of the world’s most fascinating destinations.

## 1. Shop, Shop, Shop

Absolutely top of the list of the best things to do in Dubai is shop. You’ve landed at the new international airport terminal which is the world’s largest single floor space so it’s time to make your way to Dubai Mall, the world’s largest shopping center. Opened in 2008, it has the footprint of 50+ soccer pitches. For your delectation are 1,200 shops, a cinema with 22 screens, 160 eateries and an Olympic sized ice-skating rink. One of the best places to shop for gold, jewelry, precious metals and gems, there are 200+ outlets here.  This place was also an opportunity for lots of investors who opened shops here with the help of [local attorneys](https://dubai.lawyer/).

## 2. Visit Burj Khalifa

Currently the world’s tallest building, the [Burh Khalifa](https://en.m.wikipedia.org/wiki/Burj_Khalifa) stands 2717 feet. As the signature architectural masterpiece of downtown, it’s the must-see of places to visit in Dubai. Make your way up to the observation deck on level 124 to see what looking down on the world truly feels like.

## 3. See the Grand Mosque

I’ve said see rather than visit the [Grand Mosque](https://en.m.wikipedia.org/wiki/Grand_Mosque_in_Dubai) because entrance is forbidden to non-Muslims. It wouldn’t be right to leave it off the list of things to do in Dubai because it has great local significance and is also a magnificent building even just from the outside. It boasts the tallest minaret in the city at 231 feet and has 9 large domes and 45 smaller ones.

## 4. Visit the Spectacle of Jumana

You’ll find Jumana in Dubailand out in the desert. It starts with dinner (buffet or a la carte) then it’s on to the massive outdoor auditorium which seats 1,500. You are then treated to an absolute treat of audio-visual extravagance. Jumana, the Secret of the Desert is a collage of waterworks, fireworks, dance, camels, horses and lasers held together with a voice over from Omar Sharif. Jumana certainly is one of the most impressive things to see in Dubai.

## 5. Awe at the Palm Islands

Here we go with another of the biggies. The three Palm Islands are the world’s largest man-made islands and are visible from space. Still under development, there are major projects in progress including the bridges to connect the mainland which is one of the largest construction projects in the world and then surely one of the most ambitious global aspirations – the recreation of The World on 300 small islands.

## 6. Enjoy Local Color

With all the 21st century splendour on offer, it’s important to remember some of the things to do in Dubai have a traditional flavour. Take a trip to the Heritage and Diving Villages to indulge in the Bedouin way of life. See locals in traditional dress living in barasti housing and enjoy displays of handicrafts and shows of folk song and dance.

## 7. Run out to the Hajar Mountains

Some of the popular touristy things to do in Dubai are the various 4WD tours out into the deserts and beyond. The rugged Hajar Mountains are 62 miles south of Dubai City running along the border with Oman. Enjoy great scenery including the dry river beds as you make your way to Hatta, a hill town which has been redeveloped as an oasis.

## 8. Eye up the Beautiful People

You can surely see now just why Dubai is popular with the fashionable set. The nightlife and restaurant scene is just as spectacular as everything else. Go celeb spotting at the Buddah Bar, bask in minimalistic Asian chic at Ginseng, go retro in the pyramid inspired Carter’s or be inspired by the cocktails at Left Bank. Then move on to the clubs: indulge in the decadence of the Cavalli Club, dress to impress to get into Boudoir or get down to the hip beats at The Apartment.

There is nothing shy and retiring about Dubai so it probably won’t appeal if secluded tropical beaches are your vacation scene but for a bang, all-out fest of things to do, **things to see and places to go, it’s here**. If you’re planning a trip, make an itinerary, there are **so many wonderful things to do in Dubai**, you need to choose wisely.
