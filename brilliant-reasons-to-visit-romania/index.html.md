# 7 Brilliant Reasons to Visit Romania ...

**Visit Romania**, and you’ll soon discover why this enchanting Eastern European gem has been stealing the hearts of travelers from around the globe. Whether you're an avid history buff, a nature enthusiast, or a foodie with a penchant for traditional dishes, Romania offers a unique blend of attractions that will leave you yearning for more. **Romania travel** is an adventure that promises a diverse range of experiences, each more captivating than the last.

Nestled between Hungary, Serbia, and Bulgaria, Romania is a country rich in history, culture, and natural beauty. From the hauntingly beautiful landscapes of Transylvania to the vibrant, bustling streets of [Bucharest](https://www.britannica.com/place/Bucharest/), **Romania tourism** is a treasure trove waiting to be explored. Did you know that Romania boasts some of the most impressive castles in Europe? Yes, including the legendary Bran Castle, often linked with the Dracula legend, which adds a touch of Gothic allure to your journey. The primary keyword **Romania holidays** just scratches the surface of what awaits.

Romania is often overlooked by tourists, but those in the know understand that this country is a haven for both adventure and relaxation. Imagine hiking through the [Carpathian Mountains](https://en.m.wikipedia.org/wiki/Carpathian_Mountains/), where the air is as crisp as a freshly baked pastry and the views are straight out of a postcard. Or picture yourself sipping on a glass of locally produced wine as you overlook the serene landscapes of the Danube Delta. Yes, **Romania vacations** are that versatile.

For those interested in the more modern aspects of travel, Bucharest, Romania's capital, is a must-see. With its eclectic mix of neoclassical, Bauhaus, and communist-era architecture, the city is a fascinating juxtaposition of old and new. The Old Town, in particular, is a maze of narrow cobblestone streets lined with vibrant bars, cafes, and boutiques that cater to every whim and fancy. Plus, with a burgeoning art scene and nightlife that rivals that of any European capital, **Bucharest travel** will keep you entertained day and night.

And let’s not forget the culinary delights that Romania has to offer. From hearty stews and sausages to delicate pastries and world-class wines, Romanian cuisine is a delightful fusion of flavors and traditions. You’ll find that every region has its own unique dishes and specialties, making **Romanian food** an adventure in itself.

So, whether you're planning a summer escape or a winter getaway, Romania has something to offer every traveler. With its rich history, stunning landscapes, vibrant cities, and delicious cuisine, it’s no wonder that more and more people are choosing to explore this incredible country. Buckle up and get ready for a journey that’s as educational as it is exhilarating. **Travel to Romania** and discover the magic for yourself!

## 1. Vampires

![Vampires](https://resize.allw.mn/1028x0/filters:format(webp)/filters:quality(70)/content/ef/ex/eg65oq9m_600x450.jpg)

If vampires existed then Bram Stoker’s 1897 gothic horror classic ‘Dracula’ would be the equivalent of the vampire bible. One of the best reasons to visit Romania is to admire the setting of 'Dracula,' the enchanting, mysterious Bran Castle nestled in the Carpathian Mountains. This is where the legendary, timeless vampire genre was born.

## 2. Wolves

![Wolves](https://resize.allw.mn/1028x0/filters:format(webp)/filters:quality(70)/content/dr/at/sk9ip46i_600x400.jpg)

Vampires aren't the only mysterious blood-sucking creatures roaming the Carpathian Mountains. The Romanian Carpathians are home to a large number of other European predators, including European brown bears, wolves and lynxes. So when you're there, keep an eye out for these beautiful creatures basking in the glory of their natural habitat. Be sure to stay safe and admire them from a distance.

## 3. Merry Cemetery

![Merry Cemetery](https://resize.allw.mn/1028x0/filters:format(webp)/filters:quality(70)/content/s2/d6/gbcuhr3n_600x360.jpg)

Sapanta’s Merry Cemetery breaks with tradition when it comes to the deceased. Even when Sapanta’s souls are laid to rest, they continue to kick up their heels until the end of time. Dumitri Pop carried across the wooden carved headstone tradition from mentor Ion Stan Patra. When in Romania, do yourself a favour and join in the celebration of life by paying respect to Sapanta’s more optimistic and inspirational perception of the final chapter.

## 4. Discover Decebal on the Danube

![Discover Decebal on the Danube](https://resize.allw.mn/1028x0/filters:format(webp)/filters:quality(70)/content/dv/y3/dl6ch7mc_600x400.jpg)

Brazil has Cristo, Redentor and Romania has Decebal. This magnificent 40 metre high rock sculpture of Decebal is carved in the mountain beside the Danube River. Decebal is the ancient king of Dacia and he continues to keep watch 2,000 years on of the borders he defended up until his last hour. It took twelve sculptors ten years to produce this masterpiece. Admire in awe.

## 5. Black Sea

![Black Sea](https://resize.allw.mn/1028x0/filters:format(webp)/filters:quality(70)/content/di/w8/ef4mgogd_473x338.jpg)

Romania is a country that caters to lovers of all seasons. If you are a sun seeker like me, you can soak up the surf and sand along Romani’s Black Sea coastline. In addition to good summer vibes, the Black Sea is known for its arthritic, rheumatic, and internal nervous disorder-healing properties. Make the most of your stay by splashing about in the Black Sea.

## 6. Castles and Fortresses

![Castles and Fortresses](https://resize.allw.mn/1028x0/filters:format(webp)/filters:quality(70)/content/ta/dz/yj0p83yg_600x400.jpg)

Explore the medieval era by visiting some of the castles and fortresses dotted throughout Romania’s countryside. One of my personal favorites is Transylvania’s Fagaras Fortress. This fortress is rich in history and over time has been occupied by a number of different characters, ranging from princes and their families, to communists and their political prisoners. It’s now a museum, so check it out; it even has a moat!

## 7. Trek the Carparthians

![Trek the Carparthians](https://resize.allw.mn/1028x0/filters:format(webp)/filters:quality(70)/content/w7/lk/mtsr67gb_473x338.jpg)

The Carparthian Mountains are an amazing spectacle. A number of tour companies specializing in trekking provide a truly unique experience for more active travellers. Trekking the Carparthians is challenging and not for the faint-hearted. However, if you are looking to break away from more popular treks like the Inca Trail, Annaupurna and Mount Kinabalu, then this is right up your alley.

Well there it is, my list of the seven brilliant **reasons to visit Romania**. One final point I would like to mention is Romania’s people are the heart and soul that make up this country's rich, brilliant mix of diversity, mystery and history. After all, what would a country be without its people? When visiting Romania make sure you completely immerse yourself in the experience by learning local customs, traditions and folklore. Hopefully this has inspired you to build Romania into your next European travel itinerary. Have I missed something here? Are you packing your bags yet? Give me **more reasons to visit Romania**!
