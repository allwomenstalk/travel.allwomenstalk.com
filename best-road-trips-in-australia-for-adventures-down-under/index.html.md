# 7 Best Road Trips in Australia for Adventures down under ...

If there’s one type of vacation that you really shouldn’t miss out on, it’s road trips in Australia. If you are fortunate enough to visit the land down under, getting out on the road is a brilliant way to see some of the best this magnificent country offers. The wide open road take you along superb coastlines or across massive plains. With exciting towns to stop at along the way, there’s no shortage of fun things to see and do on road trips in Australia.

## 1. Cairns to Cooktown (the Bloomfield Track)

![Cairns to Cooktown (the Bloomfield Track)](https://resize.allw.mn/1028x0/filters:format(webp)/filters:quality(70)/content/2013/10/12114204_2676_600x400.jpg)

The Cairns to Cooktown drive is one of the most challenging road trips in Australia, and can be undertaken either as a return trip along either the inland or coastal road (known as the Bloomfield Track), or as a circuit involving both. The Bloomfield Track is a well-known four-wheel drive trail, which provides tourists with stunning views of the coast although it becomes practically impassable during the wet season, whilst the inland road allows you to experience the Australian outback first-hand. If you’re interested in seeing the world renowned Daintree National Park and Cape Tribulation areas however, the Bloomfield Track is a must!

## 2. The Great Ocean Road

![The Great Ocean Road](https://resize.allw.mn/1028x0/filters:format(webp)/filters:quality(70)/content/2013/10/12114253_7764_600x450.jpg)

The Great Ocean Road which runs from Torquay to Allansford along the stunning south coast is unquestionably one of the most popular Australian road trips. Not only does this drive pass through a number of quaint seaside settlements, it also offers access to pristine beaches and waterfalls, in addition to providing travellers with unmatchable ocean views for the vast majority of the 243 kilometre stretch. The crowning glory of this road trip however, is the towering rock structures known as the 12 Apostles, which lie just of the coast in the Port Campbell National Park.

## 3. The Savannah Way

![The Savannah Way](https://resize.allw.mn/1028x0/filters:format(webp)/filters:quality(70)/content/2013/10/12114406_8954_600x447.jpg)

The Savannah Way, which stretches for 3,700 kilometres between Cairns on Australia’s east coast and Broome on the west coast, is certainly one of the must-do road trips in Australia. Incorporating numerous different biomes, in addition to 15 different national parks and numerous quaint outback towns, the Savannah Way is a must if you really want to get in touch with the “real” Australia. If you’re feeling adventurous you may also like to do a little off-road exploring in Kakadu National Park and the Kimberley Mountains.

## 4. The East Coast (Sydney to Cairns)

![The East Coast (Sydney to Cairns)](https://resize.allw.mn/1028x0/filters:format(webp)/filters:quality(70)/content/2013/10/12114534_3798_600x400.jpg)

The stretch of road between Sydney and Cairns on Australia’s east coast is by far one of the most commonly undertaken road trips in Australia. International travellers who choose to take on this 2,700 kilometre drive are generally highly impressed by the quality and quantity of the beaches, national parks and coastal settlements on offer; sometimes so much so that they choose to continue north from Cairns along the Cairns to Cooktown trail.

## 5. The Nullarbor Plain

![The Nullarbor Plain](https://resize.allw.mn/1028x0/filters:format(webp)/filters:quality(70)/content/2013/10/12114559_0036_600x450.jpg)

Without question one of the most interesting stretches of road in Australia, the Nullarbor Plain is a flat, practically treeless stretch of road, which runs between Ceduna in South Australia to Norseman in Western Australia. While this may not sound like the most exciting of Australian road trips, the Nullarbor really showcases just how vast and unforgiving Australia can be, in addition to providing travellers with great star gazing and whale watching opportunities.

## 6. The West Coast (Broome to Albany)

![The West Coast (Broome to Albany)](https://resize.allw.mn/1028x0/filters:format(webp)/filters:quality(70)/content/2013/10/12114617_8653_600x401.jpg)

The West Coast of Australia is one of the best road trips in Australia, although it is an area that is often overlooked by international travellers in preference of the east coast. What most people don’t realise however is that the 2,900 kilometre stretch of road between Broome and Albany, actually offers access to some of the most beautiful coastline in the country. Regions such as Exmouth, Monkey Mia, Shell Beach and Shark Bay are definitely guaranteed to take your breath away!

## 7. Tasmania Coast to Coast

![Tasmania Coast to Coast](https://resize.allw.mn/1028x0/filters:format(webp)/filters:quality(70)/content/2013/10/12114640_0516_600x398.jpg)

Tasmania is an area of Australia often forgotten by international tourists, although it's unquestionably one of the most stunning parts of the country. Travelers who choose to head to Tasmania during their Australian getaway however, should definitely experience the Tasmanian Coast to Coast road trip which runs from Hobart on the east coast to Strahan on the west coast. This amazing Australian road trip will draw you through luscious farmland and rainforests, in addition to providing amazing views of both the east and west coasts.
