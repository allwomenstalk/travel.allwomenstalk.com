# 8 Amazing Cities of Germany ...

Picking which amazing cities of Germany to visit would be a hard task for any visitor. Do you stick to the bustling centres like Berlin and Frankfurt or do you opt for the old world charm of cities like Heidelberg? Whatever the case, Germany has so much to offer and each of its cities offers visitors unique and memorable experiences. Check out these amazing cities of Germany.

## 1. Berlin

![Berlin](https://resize.allw.mn/1028x0/filters:format(webp)/filters:quality(70)/content/travel/2013/05/1_berlin_500x333.jpg)

Berlin is a must-see destination and one of the most amazing cities of Germany. It’s the biggest city in Germany and also its capital. While modern Berlin is a buzzing, contemporary city famous for its thriving cultural scene, it does have a sobering history. Sites like the East Side Gallery and Holocaust Memorial act as a constant reminder of a divisive past. Berlin is a great first stop for travellers.

## 2. Munich

![Munich](https://resize.allw.mn/1028x0/filters:format(webp)/filters:quality(70)/content/travel/2013/05/2_munich_600x338.jpg)

Munich is the capital of the German state of Bavaria. It is host to the famous Oktoberfest festival, which makes it a popular destination for travellers and beer lovers the world over. When the city isn’t celebrating Oktoberfest, it is home to first class museums, traditional German architecture, and plenty of picturesque scenery.

## 3. Hamburg

![Hamburg](https://resize.allw.mn/1028x0/filters:format(webp)/filters:quality(70)/content/travel/2013/05/3_hamburg_600x402.jpg)

Hamburg is the second largest city in Germany and is home to one of the biggest harbours in Europe. The city has many canals and waterways, which is why it has been nicknamed the “Venice of the North.” Hamburg is also well known for its nightlife, with over 100 music venues and clubs.

## 4. Dresden

![Dresden](https://resize.allw.mn/1028x0/filters:format(webp)/filters:quality(70)/content/travel/2013/05/4_dresden_600x399.jpg)

The city of Dresden is located in an idyllic location, spread along the picturesque banks of the Elbe River. Despite being almost completely destroyed during WWII, all of Dresden’s landmarks have been restored to their former glory. Today the city of Dresden is regaining its reputation as a city of great cultural importance.

## 5. Cologne

![Cologne](https://resize.allw.mn/1028x0/filters:format(webp)/filters:quality(70)/content/travel/2013/05/5_cologne_600x450.jpg)

Cologne is one of Germany’s oldest cities, with almost 2000 years of history to its name. While it is well known for its rich architectural history, the city also boasts a vibrant arts scene. The city is home to more than 30 museums and hundreds of galleries. Chocolate lovers might also be tempted to check out the Chocolate Museum!

## 6. Heidelberg

![Heidelberg](https://resize.allw.mn/1028x0/filters:format(webp)/filters:quality(70)/content/travel/2013/05/6_heidelberg_600x399.jpg)

Heidelberg is one of the most picturesque cities in Germany. It is also one of the few cities not to be completely destroyed during WWII. The city is home to plenty of historical landmarks, like the oldest university in Germany, and is brimming with old world charm. A stroll through the narrow cobble stone streets of the Old Town is almost like transporting back in time.

## 7. Frankfurt

![Frankfurt](https://resize.allw.mn/1028x0/filters:format(webp)/filters:quality(70)/content/travel/2013/05/7_frankfurt_600x375.jpg)

The city of Frankfurt is a mix of old and new. Gleaming skyscrapers are a relatively recent addition to the city’s 2000 year old landscape and sit alongside more traditional Gothic architecture. There’s plenty to see and do in Frankfurt – you can visit one of its many galleries, spend big on one of its many shopping streets, or head out to its large variety of clubs and restaurants. The city also hosts many different festivals throughout the year.

## 8. Stuttgart

![Stuttgart](https://resize.allw.mn/1028x0/filters:format(webp)/filters:quality(70)/content/travel/2013/05/8_stuttgart_600x400.jpg)

While some big cities can seem like a concrete jungle, Stuttgart has plenty of green spaces which make it a very festival-friendly city. Cultural types can check out the theatres and galleries while car fans can pay a visit to the Mercedes Benz Museum. The city of Stuttgart is also surrounded by one of the largest wine growing regions in Germany.

These are a few cities to visit in Germany. Have you ever been to Germany? What German city would you most like to visit?
