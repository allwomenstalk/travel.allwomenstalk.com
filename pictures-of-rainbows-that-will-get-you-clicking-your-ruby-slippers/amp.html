2 Double Rainbows

Via Departing Rainbow

When one isn't enough.

***

A double rainbow is a mesmerizing celestial phenomenon where two arcs appear simultaneously, with the second, fainter arc displaying colors in reverse order. This occurs when sunlight is reflected twice within water droplets. It's like nature's buy-one-get-one-free deal, making the sky twice as magical and inspiring a sense of wonder. Each time you glimpse this spectacle, it feels as though the world pauses and all eyes reach toward the heavens, hoping to soak in the magnificence of the moment. Double the bows, double the enchantment, and certainly, double the wishful thinking!

26 Circular Rainbow

Via All sizes | Sunrise Sunplosion ...

All rainbows are in fact circular but because the reflection is close to Earth, we only get to see an arch. We see a
circular rainbow when it forms high in the sky.

***

The full majesty of a circular rainbow is rarely witnessed unless you find yourself above the horizon—think of mountain peaks or from an airplane. The entire ring becomes visible, an ethereal spectacle of light bending in 360 degrees due to water droplets in the air, creating a moment of pure enchantment. High above, where the Earth's curvature doesn't cut off the bottom half, the sun's rays interact with moisture in just the right way, allowing lucky observers to bask in the glory of the complete circle of colors, like a halo bestowed by nature herself.

40 Richmond, Virginia

Via IMG_7068 web.jpg by Bidyottam

The trees are fighting the prismatic colors to shout loudest.

***

Richmond, Virginia is a vibrant city located on the James River in the Commonwealth of Virginia. Founded in 1737, it is the capital of Virginia and the fourth-largest city in the state. Richmond is a major cultural and financial hub, home to a number of museums, monuments, and historical sites.

Richmond is known for its beautiful natural scenery, particularly the James River, which is lined with parks, trails, and outdoor activities. Visitors to the city can explore the Virginia Museum of Fine Arts, the Virginia State Capitol, and the American Civil War Museum. The city is also home to a number of renowned restaurants, breweries, and shops.

The city is also home to a number of annual festivals and events, such as the Richmond Jazz Festival, the Richmond Folk Festival, and the Richmond International Film Festival. The city also hosts a number of sports teams, including the Richmond Flying Squirrels (baseball), the Richmond Kickers (soccer), and the Richmond Spiders (basketball).
