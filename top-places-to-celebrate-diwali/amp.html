7 Best Places to Celebrate Diwali: Your Ultimate Guide to the Festival of Lights

Diwali in Amritsar, Punjab • Diwali in Delhi • Diwali in Jaipur, Rajasthan • Diwali in Kolkata, West Bengal • Diwali in Varanasi, Uttar Pradesh • More ...

By Neecey

Diwali, aka Divali and Dhiwali, is one of the world’s most beautiful festivals. Hindus celebrate Diwali to mark the story of Lord Rama over the Demon King Ravana. Lord Rama rescued his brother Lakshman and his beloved wife Sita, who had been abducted by Ravana and to mark their return, the people lit diyas (earthen lamps), thus symbolically praising the victory of light over darkness. The word Diwali is actually derived from the Sanskrit word “Deepavali”, which translated means “row of lamps”. When people observe Diwali, it is literally, the Festival of Lights. The festival takes place in India and around the world, between mid-October and mid-December. It is a joyous occasion and there are some wonderful places to celebrate Diwali – here’s a few. Whether you are looking for the best place to celebrate Diwali or wondering about other places that celebrate Diwali, this guide will help you find the perfect destination.

1. Diwali in Amritsar, Punjab

Preparations for Diwali start usually few weeks in advance – people devotedly clean and decorate their homes with flowers and leaves, as well as with other charming items made out of blends of color and powder, known as “rangolis”. The smells of numerous dishes and sweets that will be offered to celebrate Diwali pervades the streets. Everything suggests that there is something important going on, but the culmination of the ceremony takes place on the Diwali evening, when the magical Golden Temple of Amritsar gets illuminated by thousands of diyas and candles reflecting on the water, and the lavish fireworks streak across the night sky. For photographers, this is where you can capture some of the best pictures of diwali festival and truly iconic happy diwali amritsar images. Seeing the happy diwali golden temple display is a bucket-list experience for many. Visit the official Punjab Tourism website for more travel tips.

2. Diwali in Delhi

As Rama’s wife Sita is one of the incarnations of Lakshmi, the goddess of both spiritual and material wealth, the celebration of Diwali is also being thankful for the material goods that we are blessed with. It becomes especially obvious in Delhi, in the days preceding the Diwali. People flock to commercial centers of the city and enjoy special shopping offers. Gifts, new clothes, firecrackers and sweetmeats – there are so many things to be bought to celebrate Diwali (one of the reasons the Festival of Diwali is likened to Christmas). On the very day of Diwali, the drama about the life of Lord Rama is performed on several stages across the city. If you are in the city, you will find plenty of vibrant diwali events near me that showcase the city's rich heritage.

3. Diwali in Jaipur, Rajasthan

If it is hard for you to believe that there can be more than two thousand different forms of earthen lamps, you should visit Jaipur when the city is dressed to celebrate Diwali. The market places are full with diyas in forms of various animals, birds, and, of course, cherished deities. Apart from diyas, you can also find abundance of other festive stuff, like firecrackers, local products and handicrafts. The entire Pink City glows with a golden hue, making it a top contender for the best place to celebrate Diwali in India.

4. Diwali in Kolkata, West Bengal

Kolkata has its own version of Diwali which occurs at the same time as the Kali Puja, the celebration of the goddess Kali, the Hindu goddess usually associated with the creative power. The day before Puja, the whole city is lightened with candles, colorful electric bulbs and diyas in order to celebrate the beginning of the important day at midnight. This unique cultural blend makes Kolkata one of the most fascinating places that celebrate diwali in a distinct way. Check out the West Bengal Tourism portal for event schedules.

5. Diwali in Varanasi, Uttar Pradesh

Varanasi is sometimes called “the city of lights” – are there better places to celebrate Diwali than here? Nearly one hundred Varanasi Ghats, the steps leading to holy Ganges River, are illuminated by the light of thousands of diyas, making the Diwali evening spectacular and unforgettable for all participants. The reflection of the lamps on the river creates a mystical aura that is perfect for spiritual seekers. For more information, visit the Uttar Pradesh Tourism website.

Essentials for Your Diwali Trip

  • Traditional clothing (Kurtas or Sarees) to blend in with the locals
  • A high-quality camera to capture the fireworks and illuminations
  • An appetite for traditional Indian sweets (Mithai)
  • Comfortable walking shoes for exploring the busy markets

6. Diwali in London

The celebration of light, joy and renewal is not reserved only for India and Hindus – people of all religious and national backgrounds celebrate Diwali on the streets of London, exchanging gifts and sweets and lighting hundreds of earthen lamps. It is time to cultivate family values, friendships, and other bonds within the community. There are several venues in London where you can attend various Diwali events during the festival days, including a mass gathering in Trafalgar Square. Visit the Diwali in London official site for more details on the festivities.

7. Diwali in Cape Town

The Festival of Lights has become the festival of joy of life in Cape Town – if you find yourself there at the end of October, you can enjoy a number of music events, dance performances, fashion shows and the Diwali fair, where you can taste delicious traditional dishes or buy an authentic souvenir. As Diwali is the festival of lights after all, prepare yourself for magnificent fireworks. This celebration perfectly captures the city's multicultural spirit. Explore more breathtaking places in South Africa during your visit.

Besides all the earthen lamps, candles and fireworks, we should also remember that esoterically, Diwali primarily invites us to put our own lights on. Frequently Asked Questions

Where is the best place to celebrate Diwali in India?
Delhi and Jaipur are fantastic choices with grand fireworks and traditional festivities.
Can women travel alone to celebrate Diwali safely?
Yes, many cities are safe for solo women travelers, but it's best to stay in well-reviewed accommodations and busy areas.
What should I wear to Diwali celebrations?
Traditional Indian attire like sarees or salwar kameez are perfect and help you blend in with the festive spirit.
Are there family-friendly Diwali destinations?
Yes, places like Udaipur and Mysore offer kid-friendly events and beautiful decorations.
Do I need to book accommodations early for Diwali?
Absolutely, Diwali is a peak travel time so booking early ensures better options and prices.
What local foods should women try during Diwali?
Sweets like ladoos, jalebi, and snacks like samosas are must-tries for an authentic experience.
Are there cultural events specifically women would enjoy?
Yes, many places host traditional dance performances and craft workshops ideal for women travelers.
Is it necessary to understand Hindi or local languages for Diwali trips?
Not necessary, but learning a few basic phrases can enhance your experience and interactions.
Can I buy Diwali gifts and souvenirs on my trip?
Definitely, local markets offer beautiful handicrafts and festive gifts perfect for Diwali.
What safety tips should women keep in mind during Diwali celebrations?
Stay aware of crowds, avoid isolated areas at night, and keep your belongings secure to enjoy safe celebrations.
