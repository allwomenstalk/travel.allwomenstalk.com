# 7 Marvelous Places to Go in Morocco ...

Go to places in Morocco to feel the charming spirit that merges Arabian culture with vivid African customs. A stunning coastline where the Atlantic and Mediterranean meet, magnificent Imperial cities where networks of narrow streets promise secrets to be discovered and expansive squares where life converges and clashes in a cacophony of audio and visual feasts and desert lands that whisper with mysteries of days of Saracens are all places in Morocco. This fascinating country, once a French colony, is one of the most stable of North Africa and has an established tourist industry. Check out these 7 Marvelous Places to Go in Morocco:

## 1. Fes (Fez)

![Fes (Fez)](https://resize.allw.mn/1028x0/filters:format(webp)/filters:quality(70)/content/travel/2012/05/1_fes-fez_600x428.jpg)

The oldest and the best preserved Moroccan imperial city will instantly take you back to the Middle Ages. The magnificent medina of the old Fes (Fes El Bali) is one of the most popular attractions in Morocco and is on UNESCO's World Heritage List. As an intellectual, spiritual and historical centre of Morocco, Fes has lot to offer to its visitors – Al-Karaouine, the oldest university in the world, the Jewish quarter Mellah and the Royal Palace are only some of the must-visit sights.

## 2. Chefchaouen

![Chefchaouen](https://resize.allw.mn/1028x0/filters:format(webp)/filters:quality(70)/content/travel/2012/05/2_chefchaouen_600x402.jpg)

Nested in the scenic Rif Mountains in an area interwoven with natural springs, Chefchaouen offers many possibilities for fans of the great outdoors, like hiking and swimming. Chefchaouen is also known as one of the main producers of cannabis in the country and that might be the reason why it has been one of the favorite places in Morocco for travelers from all over the globe. Take a rest at a cafe on Outa el Hammam (the main city square) and enjoy the view of the surrounding houses with doors painted in vivid colors.

## 3. Meknes

![Meknes](https://resize.allw.mn/1028x0/filters:format(webp)/filters:quality(70)/content/travel/2012/05/3_meknes_600x448.jpg)

One of the remarkable Moroccan imperial cities, Meknes was built in the 17th century by Moulay Ismail, the mighty ruler of that time. Although smaller than Fes and Marrakech, Meknes enchants with the beauty of its structures, including imposing carved gates that represent recognizable elements of Moroccan architecture. One of the places in Morocco that are well worth a visit is, for sure, Volubilis, the old Roman ruins located near Meknes.

## 4. Marrakech

![Marrakech](https://resize.allw.mn/1028x0/filters:format(webp)/filters:quality(70)/content/travel/2012/05/4_marrakech_600x449.jpg)

Shielded by the majestic Atlas Mountains, the lively city of Marrakech fascinates with its size and charm. The old imperial city is famous for its bazaars and bustling city squares, including the main square of Djemma el Fna. Enjoy the landscapes of the Marjorelle Gardens, visit the beautifully decorated Saadian Tombs and feel the traditional Moroccan lifestyle by staying in a riad, a typical local house or palace with an interior garden.

## 5. Essaouira

![Essaouira](https://resize.allw.mn/1028x0/filters:format(webp)/filters:quality(70)/content/travel/2012/05/5_essaouira_600x357.jpg)

Situated on the Atlantic coast, Essaouira boasts beautiful beaches and provides a perfect escape from the rush of the larger cities. World famous musicians Bob Marley and Jimi Hendrix enjoyed a stay in Essaouira. The extensive ramparts is one of the attractions in Morocco on UNESCO's World Heritage List. Hearing traditional Gnawash music is a must do when in Essaouira.

## 6. Tangier

![Tangier](https://resize.allw.mn/1028x0/filters:format(webp)/filters:quality(70)/content/travel/2012/05/6_tangier_600x400.jpg)

For many travelers, Tangier presents the first encounter with the African continent, as it lies on the Straits of Gibraltar. This favorable location enables tourists from Spain to visit Morocco on a day trip. The beautiful coastal city of Tangier is one of the places in Morocco with impressive Arabic architecture. A well preserved medina, the Kasbah (or fortress) and the new town (Ville Nouvelle) make Tangier a charming match of old and new, attractive to visitors from all over the world.

## 7. Dades Valley

![Dades Valley](https://resize.allw.mn/1028x0/filters:format(webp)/filters:quality(70)/content/travel/2012/05/7_dades-valley_600x395.jpg)

The Dades River flows through a valley that has been embraced by the scenic Atlas Mountains, making this place one of the most beautiful natural attractions in Morocco. Enjoy the view of traditional Moroccan forts and kasbahs scattered along the picturesque cliffs, some of which are adapted and converted into a great hotel. Dades Valley will amaze you with its Berber villages and magical landscapes that include Dades and Todra Gorges.

Beside the attractions mentioned in this short list, there are many other places in Morocco that deserve your attention. Whether you are looking for a sea resort, a winter vacation in the mountains or you simply enjoy visiting historical and cultural sites, Morocco offers all of that. Visit Moroccan medinas, drink in the bustle and scents of bazaars and, finally, explore the beautiful natural features for the overall experience of Morocco.
