# I tested 7 amazing roller coasters so you don't have to

These are all **amazing roller coasters** for summer road trips! If you’re an adrenaline junkie or you just love being whipped around curves at top speed, flipping upside down and screaming at the top of your lungs as you plummet down the track, these coasters are some of the best. Finding the ultimate thrill means seeking out the **steepest roller coaster in North America** or discovering a hidden **underground roller coaster**. The season is coming to an end, so head out soon so you can make it to all of these roller coasters for summer! Check out our related guide on [planning the perfect summer getaway](/travel/summer-destinations/).

- Experience massive vertical drops.

- Twist through underground tunnels.

- Hit top speeds in seconds.

## 1. Tallest Roller Coaster

![Tallest Roller Coaster](https://resize.allw.mn/1028x0/filters:format(webp)/filters:quality(70)/content/travel/2013/08/1_tallest-roller-coaster_600x338.jpg)

**Name:** Kingda Ka
**Location:** Six Flags Great Adventure in New Jersey

Kingda Ka is the world’s tallest roller coaster and arguably the **highest drop roller coaster in US** history, reaching a height of 456 feet! In about 3.5 seconds this coaster goes from 0 to 128 MPH, making it the fastest in the US. You will drop down through a 270 degree spiral, sure to make your breath catch in your throat! This is one coaster that definitely needs to be on your list of roller coasters for summer! You can find more details on the [official Kingda Ka page](https://www.sixflags.com/greatadventure/attractions/kingda-ka).

## 2. Longest Roller Coaster in the US

![Longest Roller Coaster in the US](https://resize.allw.mn/1028x0/filters:format(webp)/filters:quality(70)/content/travel/2013/08/2_longest-roller-coaster-in-the-us_600x442.jpg)

**Name:** Millennium Force
**Location:** Cedar Point in Ohio

Millennium Force has been ranked the #1 Steel Roller Coaster in the World 7 times in the past 12 years and any year that it was not #1, it never dropped below #2! This makes this coaster a MUST-ride for any coaster connoisseur! This awesome coaster is 6,595 feet long, which is the **longest roller coaster in the US**. If you’re outside the United States, there are 3 coasters that are a bit longer; Fujiyama and Steel Dragon 2000 in Japan are 6,709 feet and 8,133 feet respectively, leaving Steel Dragon 2000 as the longest in the world. The Ultimate in England is the runner up at 7,442 feet. For ticketing, visit the [Cedar Point website](https://www.cedarpoint.com/rides/roller-coasters/millennium-force).

## 3. Steepest Coaster in North America

![Steepest Coaster in North America](https://resize.allw.mn/1028x0/filters:format(webp)/filters:quality(70)/content/travel/2013/08/3_steepest-coaster-in-north-america_600x401.jpg)

**Name:** Steel Hawg
**Location:** Indiana Beach – Indiana

The 111 degree drop of the Steel Hawg is enough to make you feel like your internal organs are all rising up into your throat! I’m still getting used to roller coasters, so this is a little out of my league at the moment, but for all of you who just can’t get enough, this is the **steepest roller coaster in North America** that you can find! For the steepest in the world, you’ll have to travel to Japan where they have Takabisha with an overwhelming 121 degree drop! If you love steep drops, you might also enjoy reading about [how Kingda Ka reaches its top speed](#1).

| Location | Indiana, USA | Japan |
| Drop Angle | 111 degrees | 121 degrees |

## 4. Underground Coaster

![Underground Coaster](https://resize.allw.mn/1028x0/filters:format(webp)/filters:quality(70)/content/travel/2013/08/4_underground-coaster_500x333.jpg)

**Name:** Hades 360
**Location:** Mt. Olympus Water & Theme Park – Wisconsin

Face your fears of crashing to the earth as your coaster dips down. This wooden roller coaster takes you from a height of 140 feet and drops you at a 65 degree angle right into an underground tunnel! You go inside the tunnel twice, both times you are lifted off of your seat by the valleys and hills in the track. If this isn’t enough for you, there is a 360 degree barrel roll as you come out of the darkness. All of this and more from a wooden **underground roller coaster**! Usually wooden coasters are milder, but while this one does only reach 60 MPH, it makes up for a lack of top speed with unique twists, turns, and darkness! Fans often search for **the underground (roller coaster) photos** just to see its massive scale.

## 5. Spinner

![Spinner](https://resize.allw.mn/1028x0/filters:format(webp)/filters:quality(70)/content/travel/2013/08/5_spinner_500x369.jpg)

**Name:** Sierra Sidewinder
**Location:** Knott’s Berry Farm – California

While this roller coaster isn’t very fast at all and doesn’t have the enormous drops that all of the other coasters have, it does have something that is a rarity among the other coasters in the world. It is one of the very few coasters that features spinning seats! Imagine going over all of those highs and lows while spinning 360 degrees! In my opinion, this more than makes up for the lack of speed and thrill. Plan your trip with the [best theme parks in California guide](/travel/best-theme-parks-california/).

## 6. Insanity

![Insanity](https://resize.allw.mn/1028x0/filters:format(webp)/filters:quality(70)/content/travel/2013/08/6_insanity_600x475.jpg)

**Name:** Vertical Velocity
**Location:** Six Flags Great America – Illinois

This roller coaster is unlike other coasters with their constant loops and drops. The **Vertical Velocity Six Flags** ride is a U-shaped track with two vertical spikes that stick up 185 feet in the air. One is corkscrewed, causing you to twist and turn as you go up and down, while the other is straight and your coaster car sends you into a straight free fall. If fast and steep is your cup of tea, this is the coaster for you! Also known as **Six Flags V2**, it remains a fan favorite.

## 7. Intense

![Intense](https://resize.allw.mn/1028x0/filters:format(webp)/filters:quality(70)/content/travel/2013/08/7_intense_512x344.jpg)

**Name:** Bizarro
**Location:** Six Flags New England – Massachusetts

If you want a coaster that gives you as many elements as possible, Bizarro is it! This ride takes you through fog filled mini-tunnels, sets of shield shaped rings and some serious twists and turns. If that’s not enough to scare you, the ride is floorless and nearly wall-less so you have very little to hold onto or comfort you.

I hope you give some of these coasters a try. If none of them are near you, there’s bound to be some adrenaline rush-inducing rides worth traveling to around your location! Plan a mini-trip with your friends and hit as many parks as you can! What’s the most amazing coaster you’ve ever been on?
