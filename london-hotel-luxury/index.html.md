# What Makes Claridges the Epitome of Luxury and Tradition

Imagine stepping into a world where classic elegance meets contemporary luxury. This is the essence of London’s hotel scene, and if you’re planning a trip to this enchanting city, you’re in for a treat. Not only does London offer some of the best sights and sounds but also a collection of magnificent hotels that promise unforgettable experiences.

One name that stands out in the realm of hotel luxury is [Oliver Thornton Home](https://www.instagram.com/oliverthorntonhome/), whose exquisite sense of design encapsulates the distinct character of each establishment. From the historic charm of Hazlitts to the avant-garde elegance of The Beaumont, the craftsmanship is evident in every corner. Hotels like Claridges and The NoMad are more than just places to stay - they are experiences in themselves, each with unique stories and design philosophies. Whether you’re lounging in a beautifully appointed room enveloped in rich textures or savoring a delicious meal in a thoughtfully designed restaurant, these places hold the magic of London’s storied past while offering a glimpse into the future.

Continue reading as we journey through these breathtaking spaces and discover their stories captured in stunning images.

## At Sloane: A Taste of Timeless Elegance

**Francsois-Joseph Graf** offers a design that is both grand yet inviting at At Sloane. The ornate décor tells a story of history while maintaining a fresh, contemporary appeal.

![image](https://img.allw.mn/uploads/cd9e5ebc-563f-4d08-8c48-c7e8ccbebae0_1080x1440.jpg)

Photo: @oliverthorntonhome

Parisian art-deco mixes with the charm of West London, with rooms priced around $400. This splendid haven invites guests to lose themselves in its luxurious embrace.

## Claridges: Where Luxury Meets Tradition

The interiors of Claridges, designed by Bryan O’Sullivan, are the epitome of sophistication and luxury. The marble, soft lighting, and fine dining create an atmosphere where guests can truly feel pampered.

![image](https://img.allw.mn/uploads/a2850698-3e0d-421b-99e4-c0e09558dc17_1080x1440.jpg)

Photo: @oliverthorntonhome

A night here is a splurge at about $900, yet with its exquisite service and ambiance, it's worth every penny.

![image](https://img.allw.mn/uploads/a7bbd5fb-2ce1-463b-bd8c-7f93a195fa61_1080x1439.jpg)

Photo: @oliverthorntonhome

The hotel’s bar is equally decadent and perhaps a perfect spot for evening gatherings with loved ones - an experience to behold.

## Henry’s Townhouse: Quintessentially British

At Henry’s Townhouse, designed by Russell Sage Studio, you’ll find a cozy and quintessentially British charm brimming in every corner. This hotel speaks to those with a penchant for history and elegance.

![image](https://img.allw.mn/uploads/14da74a8-ec51-4b4c-bc1a-6bd8e1e8f547_1080x1439.jpg)

Photo: @oliverthorntonhome

Priced around $250, guests are offered a unique, intimate atmosphere that feels like home.

![image](https://img.allw.mn/uploads/272c01aa-466d-4fa9-ad4a-b6343bbe4cbc_1080x1440.jpg)

Photo: @oliverthorntonhome

The rooms are an escape into luxurious vintage, where every detail, from the soft linens to the glorious chandeliers, is meant to enchant.

## The Beaumont: A Retreat unto Itself

Thierry Despont’s design at The Beaumont caters to those yearning for something opulent yet understated. It’s both a retreat and a temple to the timeless chic of the 1920s.

![image](https://img.allw.mn/uploads/1ca754f7-d2f2-4997-bc6f-3e7be52d77e4_1000x1333.jpg)

Photo: @oliverthorntonhome

A room here typically goes for $600, offering guests an opportunity to revel in tranquility.

## Hazlitts & The Rookery: A Touch of History

For those drawn to the intimacy of period character, Hazlitts, with its timeless appeal, presents Victorian elegance through and through.

![image](https://img.allw.mn/uploads/bd2bd3f4-c262-4d2e-92aa-727cfbce783d_1080x1440.jpg)

Photo: @oliverthorntonhome

Crafted with care, rooms average around $180, presenting a serene and authentic alternative to the larger hotel experiences.

## The NoMad: New York Meets London

The NoMad's restaurant, designed by Roman & Williams, unveils an interior where New York flair blends with London sophistication.

![image](https://img.allw.mn/uploads/0a1cb634-80ff-4bd9-b953-2f6d605f46f2_1080x1440.jpg)

Photo: @oliverthorntonhome

Dining in this illuminating space is itself a feast for the senses, and with entrees starting at $50, guests can indulge comfortably.

![image](https://img.allw.mn/uploads/2a35851a-cf50-4761-be7a-cda7e07736b2_767x1022.jpg)

Photo: @oliverthorntonhome

Every nook is an invitation to discover another layer of luxury and culture.

## Covent Garden Hotel: A Designer’s Oasis

**Kit Kemp’s** design work makes Covent Garden Hotel a standout with vibrant, eclectic décor.

![image](https://img.allw.mn/uploads/1cec670e-977f-41b4-8689-f30fc567f757_1080x1440.jpg)

Photo: @oliverthorntonhome

Rooms start at $500, embodying a bright and joyful luxury that echoes the spirited area of Covent Garden itself.

## The Cadogan Hotel: A Fusion of Classic and Modern

GA Design's work for The Cadogan Hotel’s lobby infuses elegant contemporary touches within a classic setting.

![image](https://img.allw.mn/uploads/31e98881-410a-4a61-8097-2d99a021a550_1080x1440.jpg)

Photo: @oliverthorntonhome

Priced at around $450 per night, this graceful blending of styles makes every visit feel special, whether it be a relaxed stay or a business visit.
