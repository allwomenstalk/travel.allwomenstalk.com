# 9 Spooky Haunted Places in Louisiana to Explore This Year

If you and I were on a road trip through the south, we would totally hit up a few haunted places in Louisiana. We would travel from the northwest part of the state, around Shreveport, and down into the southeast area of New Orleans. I'm not sure how long the route would take, but the trip would be well worth it to see a few haunted places in Louisiana and get a good scare. There is so much history to explore; why not mix a little road-tripping through historical sites with an interest in the spooky side of Louisiana lore? If you're planning your own journey, you might also want to check out some [fun facts about Louisiana](https://travel.allwomenstalk.com/fun-facts-about-louisiana-you-might-not-know/) to round out your itinerary.

## 1. The Old Ellerbe Road School, Shreveport

![The Old Ellerbe Road School, Shreveport](https://resize.allw.mn/1028x0/filters:format(webp)/filters:quality(70)/content/a2/vw/q4/ps7z8zsm_600x400.jpg)

First up on our brief trip through haunted places in Louisiana is the Old Ellerbe Road School. Originally built in 1957 as the George Washington Carver High School, the structure was abandoned in 1985 and remains empty more than twenty years later. This setting is perfect for the spooky tales that surround it. The main legend of this place indicates that a janitor abducted and murdered some students before disappearing himself. There are a few versions of this tale. However, visitors to the area report similar experiences: disembodied laughter of children, crazed laughter of a madman, evidence of rituals and shadows.

## 2. The Magnolia Plantation, Natchitoches

![The Magnolia Plantation, Natchitoches](https://resize.allw.mn/1028x0/filters:format(webp)/filters:quality(70)/content/vf/rm/kxbu8lh7_600x450.jpg)

There are many plantation homes still standing across the American south, and Louisiana is no exception. Each one has its own history and story to tell. Our trip south stops at the [Magnolia Plantation](https://www.nps.gov/cari/learn/historyculture/magnolia-plantation.htm). Many people were tortured and the overseer was later killed by Union soldiers on this land. This horrible history still haunts the grounds today. Visitors to the Magnolia Plantation report seeing the ghosts of former residents and soldiers as well as that of the overseer. Visitors also report hearing disembodied voices.

## 3. Alexandria Zoological Park, Alexandria

![Alexandria Zoological Park, Alexandria](https://resize.allw.mn/1028x0/filters:format(webp)/filters:quality(70)/content/nr/tt/hn/rrp9pp2i_600x450.jpg)

Have you ever heard of a haunted zoo? South of Magnolia Plantation is the [Alexandria Zoological Park](https://www.thealexandriazoo.com/). The park is said to be haunted by the ghost of a former park Director. In life the Park Director loved to play pranks and practical jokes on his staff. After passing away in 2008, it seems the Director continues this naughty behavior today. Workers report seeing his ghost from time to time as well as experiencing pranks and jokes similar to those the Director played on them while still alive.

## 4. Harris Hall at University of Louisiana, Lafayette

![Harris Hall at University of Louisiana, Lafayette](https://resize.allw.mn/1028x0/filters:format(webp)/filters:quality(70)/content/nz/rk/oi/j8ykt5v7_600x450.jpg)

Lafayette, Louisiana is home of the University of Louisiana. On these grounds stands the understated women's dormitory, Harris Hall. Local legend tells the story of a woman who was crushed by a faulty elevator. The tragic story is replayed every night around the time she is said to have died. Security and residents report hearing the elevator doors open and close when no one is around. No need to be too afraid as the ghost is said to be friendly, helping people find missing items. If you're visiting the campus, you might also be interested in other [haunted college campuses](https://lifestyle.allwomenstalk.com/haunted-college-campuses-around-the-us/) across the country.

## 5. Joseph Jefferson Mansion, New Iberia

![Joseph Jefferson Mansion, New Iberia](https://resize.allw.mn/1028x0/filters:format(webp)/filters:quality(70)/content/oh/qd/07/cp4wxba7_600x376.jpg)

The serene looks of this 1870s era home is a little deceiving because the [Joseph Jefferson Mansion](http://www.ripvanwinklegardens.com/) is reportedly alive with paranormal activity. Tales include stories of long-dead pirates keeping watch over buried treasure and the lingering ghosts of grounds keepers minding their duties well after death. Visitors also report seeing the ghosts of a little girl and a tall woman roam the halls. Perhaps the most well-known ghostly figure seen at Joseph Jefferson Mansion is that of Jefferson himself. Visitors report seeing his ghost watching the grounds from his upper window. This would totally be a stop on our spine-chilling haunted places list!

## 6. Myrtles Plantation, St. Francisville

![Myrtles Plantation, St. Francisville](https://resize.allw.mn/1028x0/filters:format(webp)/filters:quality(70)/content/05/qb/0a/u9y6tia4_600x450.jpg)

Built in 1796, [Myrtles Plantation](https://www.myrtlesplantation.com/) is one of the most haunted places in the country. You see, this lovely home has a horrible past that still echoes today. Local legend reports that a mother and her two children, and their house slave, Chloe, died here. Chloe was a tortured slave who enacted revenge on the person responsible for her injuries. To this end, Chloe poisoned a cake, killing his wife and two children. Although Chloe was hanged as punishment for this crime, she has not left. Visitors to the plantation report seeing her ghost around the property and in their rooms at night. Her image was captured on film in an infamous photo outside the home. Would you sleep here?

## 7. Old State Penitentiary, Baton Rouge

![Old State Penitentiary, Baton Rouge](https://resize.allw.mn/1028x0/filters:format(webp)/filters:quality(70)/content/sx/lo/xs/dob7izft_600x450.jpg)

A trip to Louisiana would not be complete without a stop in Baton Rouge and New Orleans. The state capital of Baton Rouge is home to the Old State Penitentiary. Built in the late nineteenth century, this old building now houses the Louisiana State police headquarters and barracks. Offices now stand in areas that once housed a prison morgue and execution room. Yikes! Employees and visitors to the museum area report hearing footsteps when no one is around and experiencing severe electrical issues with equipment turning on and off on its own. The many reports of activity and paranormal investigations mark the Old State Penitentiary as a hotspot of activity.

## 8. LaLaurie Mansion, New Orleans

![LaLaurie Mansion, New Orleans](https://resize.allw.mn/1028x0/filters:format(webp)/filters:quality(70)/content/as/na/nw/ml14x95j_550x412.jpg)

Built in the late eighteenth century, the house now known as LaLaurie mansion has a truly horrifying history. One night, as her home burned, Madame LaLaurie fled Louisiana. When the home was opened, slaves were found chained and mutilated. A veritable chamber of horrors was found on the upper floor in which slaves were tortured, dismembered, and experimented upon. More bodies were found buried on the property and thrown inside a well. Today the home is a popular tourist destination. Visitors report hearing disembodied voices and seeing apparitions on the property. Furniture moves on its own and unseen hands touch visitors.

## 9. Marie Laveau House, New Orleans

![Marie Laveau House, New Orleans](https://resize.allw.mn/1028x0/filters:format(webp)/filters:quality(70)/content/ff/aw/t0/vwydq6kz_600x402.jpg)

Like LaLaurie Mansion, Marie Laveau's house is a haunted place to visit while in Louisiana. Some people would say that it is a paranormal can't-miss! If legends are true, then this little place is incredibly active. Marie Laveau was a well-known voodoo priestess. Many people, from the very young to the old and powerful, sought her services then and continue to do so today. Visitors to Marie Laveau's house report hearing ritual sounds and seeing Marie's image around town.

When visiting these locations, keep in mind these quick safety and etiquette tips:

- Always respect private property and local laws.

- Check visiting hours and booking requirements in advance.

- Stay with your group and avoid venturing into unsafe structures.

- Be mindful of the historical significance and sensitive nature of these sites.
