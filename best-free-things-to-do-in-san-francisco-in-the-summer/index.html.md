# 7 Best Free Things to Do in San Francisco This Summer: A Budget\-Friendly Guide

No ideas of where to go for summer vacation? Find a cheap flight and head off to the sunny shores of California and the City by the Bay. There are some great free things to do in San Francisco, from world-class museums to iconic landmarks. If you’re looking for a city with the bonus of being on the ocean where you can have an action-packed itinerary that won’t cost you a year’s salary, San Fran is it. Whether you are [exploring San Francisco on a budget](https://travel.allwomenstalk.com/how-to-travel-san-francisco-on-a-budget/) or just looking for local secrets, take a look at some of these great ways to holiday without breaking the bank.

## 1. Free Attractions

![Free Attractions](https://resize.allw.mn/1028x0/filters:format(webp)/filters:quality(70)/content/wh/lf/maqlcqzw5523e05b488f4728283481_500x345.jpg)

Of all the free things to do in San Francisco, walking across the 1.7-mile [Golden Gate Bridge](https://www.goldengate.org/) is one of the most popular attractions. It’s free, open to foot and bike traffic during daylight hours, and offers striking views of the Pacific and the city skyline. You could also take a free tour with [San Francisco City Guides](https://sfcityguides.org/), exploring a number of historical buildings and neighborhoods. Ghirardelli Square costs nothing, and you might even get a free piece of chocolate. If you like history, visit the San Francisco Maritime National Historical Park, where you can see historical ships, or the Cable Car Museum where you can see antique cable cars, an important part of San Francisco’s transportation history.

## 2. Free Food and Drink

![Free Food and Drink](https://resize.allw.mn/1028x0/filters:format(webp)/filters:quality(70)/content/ss/t5/ld30qekx5523e11f5507b834358727_600x399.jpg)

Well, it’s not 100% free; you usually have to buy something to get the bonus treats. But it’s still a great deal! For example, if you head to [Palio](https://www.paliosf.com/) (formerly Palio D’Asti) during happy hour, you can enjoy wood-fired pizzas with your drinks. While old favorites like Liverpool Lil’s and Maya’s have closed, you can find a similar old-school vibe and great deals at **The Brazen Head** or enjoy happy hour margaritas at **The Matador**. For wine lovers, check out the [Winery Collective](https://winerycollective.com/) in Fisherman's Wharf, which often features local flights. You might spend a few dollars on a signature cocktail, but the complimentary snacks and atmosphere make it well worth the visit.

## 3. Free Art

![Free Art](https://resize.allw.mn/1028x0/filters:format(webp)/filters:quality(70)/content/hc/de/sjxjrlo65523e330e8db9511481633_600x412.jpg)

If you arrive near the first Tuesday of the month, the [San Francisco Museum of Modern Art (SFMOMA)](https://www.sfmoma.org/) often has community days, and it is well worth the time. Exhibits include paintings by artists such as Frida Kahlo and Henri Matisse, along with photography and design. The [Asian Art Museum](https://asianart.org/) is also open for free on the first Sunday of the month, and there you can see the oldest known Chinese Buddha. On the second Sunday of the month, visit the [Oakland Museum of California](https://museumca.org/) for free. Along with California art, you can walk through 9 distinct ecosystems in the Natural Sciences Gallery.

## 4. Free Music

![Free Music](https://resize.allw.mn/1028x0/filters:format(webp)/filters:quality(70)/content/g4/y0/rk8mkvnz5523e6c051fd1769425039_600x399.jpg)

Watch the [Golden Gate Park Band](https://goldengateparkband.org/) all summer for free at Spreckel’s Temple of Music, or visit one of the many free concerts offered by the [San Francisco Chamber Orchestra](https://www.sfchamberorchestra.org/). The director will lead talks about the music for a half-hour before each show. Throughout the summer, the [Stern Grove Festival](https://www.sterngrove.org/) offers incredible free outdoor concerts featuring everything from symphony music to modern rock. It's the perfect way to spend a sunny Sunday afternoon in the city.

## 5. Free Theater

![Free Theater](https://resize.allw.mn/1028x0/filters:format(webp)/filters:quality(70)/content/rs/sg/pfda0ooh5523ea60f144f967469846_600x400.jpg)

While some repertory companies like the San Francisco Free Civic Theatre have become dormant, the city still hums with live performance. Check out the [SF LIVE](https://www.sflivefest.com/) series for free outdoor activations and concerts. Free plays courtesy of [Shakespeare in the Park](https://www.sfshakes.org/) offer professional theater under the backdrop of the stars. These plays occur at parks all throughout the city, beginning in June and ending in late September. It’s a wonderful way to experience [different San Francisco neighborhoods](https://travel.allwomenstalk.com/essential-san-francisco-neighborhoods-to-explore/) while enjoying high-quality art.

## 6. Free Gardens

![Free Gardens](https://resize.allw.mn/1028x0/filters:format(webp)/filters:quality(70)/content/c7/i4/dhhwgl895523eac4c431a760279972_520x307.jpg)

Visit the [San Francisco Botanical Garden](https://www.sfbg.org/) for free and get acquainted with more than 16,000 plants over 55 acres. There are several interesting collections, including several areas focused on specific genera and others focused on certain environments. The tours are also complimentary and are offered seven days a week. The [Yerba Buena Gardens](https://yerbabuenagardens.com/) is also free, and while it is much smaller, it is still awesome. At two-and-a-half acres, it features a waterfall, a memorial to Martin Luther King Jr., and—if you want to spend a little money—a carousel, a skating rink, and bowling.

## 7. Free Culture

![Free Culture](https://resize.allw.mn/1028x0/filters:format(webp)/filters:quality(70)/content/c1/d9/oq6bf0e05523eb9f997f1116317431_600x402.jpg)

If you’re looking to get a little culture for free, then San Francisco is your city. Here you’ll find the [Chinatown Night Market Fair](https://www.bechinatown.com/nightmarket), offering authentic Chinese food, music, and entertainment on the second Friday of each month. The San Francisco Center for the Book lets you learn the history and art of bookmaking, and if you head off to the Museo ItaloAmericano, you’ll learn about the Italian contribution to society. Other free events include the Haight-Ashbury Street Fair and the [San Francisco Free Folk Festival](https://sffolkfest.org/).

### Budget Travel Tips for SF

- **Layer Up:** The fog (affectionately known as Karl) can roll in quickly, so always bring a jacket.

- **Walk More:** Many neighborhoods like North Beach and Chinatown are best explored on foot.

- **Use the App:** Download the MuniMobile app for the best rates on public transportation.

Remember too that sightseeing on foot costs nothing. You can walk to Alamo Square to see the famous [Painted Ladies](https://travel.allwomenstalk.com/magnificent-museums-to-visit-in-san-francisco/), visit Chinatown for a taste of the Orient, and wander the vibrant Mission District. San Fran won’t let you down.

Did you imagine there’d be so many fabulous free things to do in San Francisco? Pack your layers and get ready for a budget-friendly adventure!
