# 7 Amazing Places You Should Visit in Monaco ...

There are a number of great places to visit in Monaco. The principality of Monaco is located on the French Riviera, making it a picturesque travel destination. Despite it being fairly small in size, there is plenty to see and do in Monaco. While it is probably best known for its Monte-Carlo casinos, there are plenty of other destinations worth checking out if you ever find yourself in the region. Take a look at these great places to visit in Monaco.

## 1. Palais Du Prince

![Palais Du Prince](https://resize.allw.mn/1028x0/filters:format(webp)/filters:quality(70)/content/travel/2013/06/1_palais-du-prince_500x375.jpg)

One of the top places to visit in Monaco is the Prince’s Palace. It is the official residence of the Prince of Monaco and is located in Monaco-Ville. The state rooms are open to the public during the summer months and entry tickets to the palace also include admission into the Napoleon Museum next door. Even if you don’t take a tour inside, you can catch the changing of the guards that takes place just before noon each day.

## 2. Monte-Carlo Casino

![Monte-Carlo Casino](https://resize.allw.mn/1028x0/filters:format(webp)/filters:quality(70)/content/travel/2013/06/2_monte-carlo-casino_600x450.jpg)

If you’re old enough, the Monte-Carlo Casino is worth a visit. Unlike the flashy casinos of Vegas, the Monte-Carlo Casino is all class. Even if you’re not a gambler, it’s worth dressing up and stepping inside, just to see the lavish décor and experience how the high rollers live.

## 3. Jardin Exotique

![Jardin Exotique](https://resize.allw.mn/1028x0/filters:format(webp)/filters:quality(70)/content/travel/2013/06/3_jardin-exotique_400x300.jpg)

For such a small region, Monaco seems to have a lot of gardens. One of the must-sees is the Jardin Exotique, or the exotic gardens. It’s located on a cliffside and you can grab some of the best views of Monaco from the gardens. Enjoy a stroll through the collections of succulents and cacti, and then head down for a tour of La Grotte de l’Observatoire – a deep mountainside cave located at the foot of the cliff.

## 4. Cathédrale De Monaco

![Cathédrale De Monaco](https://resize.allw.mn/1028x0/filters:format(webp)/filters:quality(70)/content/travel/2013/06/4_cath-drale-de-monaco_600x399.jpg)

Located in the old town of Monaco, Cathédrale de Monaco is a popular destination for visitors. While the cathedral is the resting place for many royals, the most notable are the flower-covered graves of Princess Grace and Prince Rainier III.

## 5. Grimaldi Forum

![Grimaldi Forum](https://resize.allw.mn/1028x0/filters:format(webp)/filters:quality(70)/content/travel/2013/06/5_grimaldi-forum_600x400.jpg)

While it is Monaco’s congress centre, the Grimaldi Forum is also a cultural centre. Check out a ballet performance by Les Ballets des Monte-Carlo, Monaco’s premier dance company, or enjoy contemporary art exhibitions that frequently take place there. The building itself is also worth a look – two-thirds of it is submerged in the sea and it’s full of designer dining and drinking spaces.

## 6. Musée Océanographique

![Musée Océanographique](https://resize.allw.mn/1028x0/filters:format(webp)/filters:quality(70)/content/travel/2013/06/6_mus-e-oc-anographique_500x334.jpg)

The Musee Oceanographique, or the Oceanographic Museum, is one of Monaco’s best museums. Built into a cliff in the back of the Old Town, the location alone is worth checking out! The top two floors of the museum are dedicated to ocean exhibitions, while down below you’ll find an aquarium with tanks full of sharks, exotic fish, and coral reefs.

## 7. Roseraie Princess Grace

![Roseraie Princess Grace](https://resize.allw.mn/1028x0/filters:format(webp)/filters:quality(70)/content/travel/2013/06/7_roseraie-princess-grace_600x449.jpg)

The Roseraie Princess Grace, or the Princess Grace Rose Garden, is located in the Fontvieille district of Monaco. The rose garden was created in 1984 as a memorial to Princess Grace of Monaco. With over 4000 rose bushes and a small swan-filled lake, a stroll through this picturesque garden is definitely worthwhile.
