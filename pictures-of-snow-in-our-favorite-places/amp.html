1 Swallow's Nest Castle, Ukraine

Via castle

Pictures of snow and castles are always a favorite. This one is on Ukraine's Black Sea Coast, overlooking the spa town of Gaspra.

***

The Swallow's Nest Castle seems to emerge from a fairy tale, especially when its turrets and battlements are dusted with a delicate layer of snow. Perched atop the 40-meter high Aurora Cliff, visitors get panoramic views that are absolutely breathtaking in winter, enhancing the romance of the site. It's the perfect backdrop for those whimsical moments, captured in photos that whisper the history of the Crimean coastline. Whether blanketed in white or just caught in the pale winter sun, this architectural gem presents the magic of the season like no other.

32 The Cotswolds, England

Via Cotswolds

This part of England is full of chocolate box villages.

***

The Cotswolds is an area of Outstanding Natural Beauty in the south of England, full of picturesque villages and rolling hills. It is known for its honey-colored stone buildings, traditional cottages, and charming country pubs.

The Cotswolds is a great place to visit in the winter when the countryside is blanketed in snow. The scenery is breathtaking, with snow-covered hills and valleys, and quaint villages tucked away in the valleys.

The Cotswolds is also a great place to explore in the summer months. The countryside is full of wildflowers and wildlife, and the villages are full of antiques shops, art galleries, and tea rooms. There are plenty of activities to enjoy, such as walking, cycling, and horse riding.

The Cotswolds is home to many historic sites, such as the Roman Fosse Way, the Cotswold Way, and the Cotswold Water Park. The Cotswolds is also home to some of the country's best gardens, including the National Trust's Hidcote Manor Garden.

56 Kyoto, Japan

Via [INFP] INFP "porn" - Page ...

Kinkaku-ji Buddhist Temple - The Temple of the Golden Pavilion

***

Kinkaku-ji Buddhist Temple, located in Kyoto, Japan, is a UNESCO World Heritage Site and one of the most popular tourist attractions in the country. The Temple of the Golden Pavilion is an ornate three-story building, covered in gold leaf and surrounded by a beautiful garden. It was originally built in 1397 as a retirement villa for the shogun Ashikaga Yoshimitsu, and later converted into a Buddhist temple. The temple is also home to a number of important artifacts, including a statue of the Buddhist goddess Kannon and a painting of the Amida Buddha. Visitors to the temple can enjoy the peaceful atmosphere and take in the stunning views of the surrounding area.
