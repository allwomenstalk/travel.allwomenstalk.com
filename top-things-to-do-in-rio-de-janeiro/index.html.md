# 10 Top Things to do in Rio De Janeiro ...

There are so many things to do in Rio de Janeiro. It’s a fantastic holiday destination filled with fun, sun, culture and soccer. It’s Brazil’s biggest tourist attraction, so its facilities cater meticulously to showing its visitors a great time. There are so many things to do in Rio - It hosts around 2.82 million tourists per year, has a plethora of luxurious hotels and is a thriving cultural hub. If you’re planning on going, following these10 Top Things to do in Rio de Janeiro are a must.

## 1. Walk through the Botanical Gardens

![Walk through the Botanical Gardens](https://resize.allw.mn/1028x0/filters:format(webp)/filters:quality(70)/content/travel/2012/09/1_walk-through-the-botanical-gardens_600x399.jpg)

These gardens are absolutely stunning. Even non-nature lovers will be astounded by the aestheticism behind beautiful flowers and luscious greenery. It’s a wonderful experience to walk through the garden’s several winding paths which each take you to a differently themed area. One of the most tranquil places to go in Rio de Janeiro, be sure to see the Sensorial Garden and Orquidario.

## 2. Explore the Bohemian Neighborhood of Santa Teresa

![Explore the Bohemian Neighborhood of Santa Teresa](https://resize.allw.mn/1028x0/filters:format(webp)/filters:quality(70)/content/travel/2012/09/2_explore-the-bohemian-neighborhood-of-santa-teresa_600x400.jpg)

This intriguing neighborhood is unique and a visit is one of the best things to do in Rio de Janeiro. It overlooks the city and is home to many of Brazil’s most talented artists, who set up shop in the 1960s. Go day or night, either way you’ll see some spectacular sights. From art expos to nightclubs, from cafes to sambas, Santa Teresa will show you a great time (just make sure you never travel alone at night for safety’s sake).

## 3. Lounge around on Ipanema Beach

![Lounge around on Ipanema Beach](https://resize.allw.mn/1028x0/filters:format(webp)/filters:quality(70)/content/travel/2012/09/3_lounge-around-on-ipanema-beach_600x398.jpg)

One of the legendary attractions of Rio, it’s easy to spend all day at this spectacular beach. It stretches from Leblon to Leme, crossing Ipanema and Copacabana. It’s divided into sections and each one has their own unique characteristics. If you love surfing, it’s best to go to the sections that are near the rocks where the local surfers hang out.

## 4. Hike through Tijuca National Park

![Hike through Tijuca National Park](https://resize.allw.mn/1028x0/filters:format(webp)/filters:quality(70)/content/travel/2012/09/4_hike-through-tijuca-national-park_600x400.jpg)

This National Park hosts the world’s largest urban forest. If you don’t want to go to the beach, this is one of the great alternative places to go in Rio de Janeiro. There are plenty of paths and stimulating waterfalls for you to take lovely pictures during your hike whilst you explore this natural park.

## 5. If It Rains, Spend a Day at the Banco do Brazil Cultural Center

![If It Rains, Spend a Day at the Banco do Brazil Cultural Center](https://resize.allw.mn/1028x0/filters:format(webp)/filters:quality(70)/content/travel/2012/09/5_if-it-rains-spend-a-day-at-the-banco-do-brazil-cultural-center_600x575.jpg)

If you don’t want to be cooped up in your accommodation just because of bad weather, the Cultural center is one of the interesting things to do in Rio de Janeiro. It’s a spectacular building that puts on exhibitions, movies and theater shows. Its name comes from the fact that it housed the headquarters of the Banco do Brazil in 1923.

## 6. Play Beach Volleyball with the Locals

![Play Beach Volleyball with the Locals](https://resize.allw.mn/1028x0/filters:format(webp)/filters:quality(70)/content/travel/2012/09/6_play-beach-volleyball-with-the-locals_600x387.jpg)

Feel up for some action and competition? Strike up an impromptu game of beach volleyball with the locals who’re sure to give you a challenge in this game invented by Brazilians. Volleyball nets can be found on most beaches, particularly those between Paula Freitas and Hilario de Gouveia.

## 7. Kite or Windsurfing

![Kite or Windsurfing](https://resize.allw.mn/1028x0/filters:format(webp)/filters:quality(70)/content/travel/2012/09/7_kite-or-windsurfing_600x380.jpg)

Lovers of the outdoors will find plenty of vacation activities in Rio de Janeiro. Kite and wind surfing is a favorite pastime on the coast, particularly at Barra beach because it is particularly windy with good strong waves. It’s recommended that you start near the Barraca do Pepȇ kiosk for the greatest experience.

## 8. Go Dancing at the Sambodromo

![Go Dancing at the Sambodromo](https://resize.allw.mn/1028x0/filters:format(webp)/filters:quality(70)/content/travel/2012/09/8_go-dancing-at-the-sambodromo_600x366.jpg)

The Sambodromo is the heart of Rio de Janeiro’s Carnaval and has been for every year since 1984. If you’re lucky enough to be in Rio when music concerts are on, you must definitely go. Their concerts run throughout the year, and have lots of music concerts leading up to the Carnaval.

## 9. Soak up the Views from Corcovado and Pão De açúcar

![Soak up the Views from Corcovado and Pão De açúcar](https://resize.allw.mn/1028x0/filters:format(webp)/filters:quality(70)/content/travel/2012/09/9_soak-up-the-views-from-corcovado-and-p-o-de-a-car_600x375.jpg)

Both of these vantage points offer incredible views of Rio. You can get to Corcovado by taking a train ride through Cosme Velho. Then, go towards Sugarloaf to take a cable car at Praia Vermelha that takes you straight to the top, letting you peacefully gaze at your beautiful surroundings. Timing it so you soar through the sky during sunset is one of the top things to do in Rio de Janeiro.

## 10. If You Need a Break from Rio’s Urban Scene, Go to Parque Lage

![If You Need a Break from Rio’s Urban Scene, Go to Parque Lage](https://resize.allw.mn/1028x0/filters:format(webp)/filters:quality(70)/content/travel/2012/09/10_if-you-need-a-break-from-rios-urban-scene-go-to-parque-lage_600x396.jpg)

Parque Lage is a mansion nestled in the shadow of Corcovado Mountain. Its gorgeous gardens were built by Henrique Lage to show his intense love for the Italian opera singer Gabriela Bezzanzoni. The front gate of this mansion is filled with leafy vegetation that instantly transports you away from Rio’s urban land. Go here for some [gourmet food](https://food.allwomenstalk.com/easy-gourmet-dinners-for-tonight/), peace, quiet and pleasing foliage.

I hope you’re tempted by some of these things to do in Rio de Janeiro. They are unique and breathtaking experiences which any tourist travelling to Brazil can’t miss.
