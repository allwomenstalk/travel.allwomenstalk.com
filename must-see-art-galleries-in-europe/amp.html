10 Must-See Art Galleries in Europe for Culture Lovers

Musée Du Louvre • Museo Del Prado • Musée National D'Art Moderne • Victoria and Albert Museum • Tate Modern • More ...

By Sophia

The art galleries in Europe are some of the most visited in the world. There are many famous art galleries in Europe and most of them are popular tourist attractions. If you’re ever lucky enough to travel through Europe, a visit to any of the major art galleries will have you viewing masterpieces from throughout the ages. These are just a few of the must-see art galleries in Europe for every culture seeker.

Gallery NameLocationMain Attraction
Musée du LouvreParis, FranceMona Lisa

1. Musée Du Louvre

The Louvre is without a doubt one of the most popular art galleries in Europe, possibly even the world. It attracts more than 8 million visitors a year and is home to thousands of priceless paintings and antiquities, with the most famous being the portrait of the Mona Lisa. The Louvre is a Parisian icon and a must-see upon visiting the city. If you are planning a trip, don't forget to check out our packing tips for a European vacation to ensure you're prepared for long museum walks.

2. Museo Del Prado

The Museo del Prado is located in central Madrid, Spain. It is the main Spanish national art museum and holds one of the world’s finest collections of European art. Much of the collection comes from Spanish royalty and features many works by famous Spanish artists like Goya and Velázquez.

3. Musée National D'Art Moderne

If you prefer modern art then check out the Musée National d'Art Moderne. It is located in Paris, France and is the largest modern art gallery in Europe. It also has the second largest collection of modern art in the world, making it a cornerstone for anyone interested in 20th-century movements.

4. Victoria and Albert Museum

The Victoria and Albert Museum is located in central London. It is the world’s largest museum of decorative arts and design. Inside the art gallery you’ll find art spanning 5000 years, from ancient times to the present day. The collections hold pieces from all over the world and cover everything from architecture, jewellery, fashion, photography, and sculpture. For more inspiration, see our guide on free things to do in London.

5. Tate Modern

The Tate Modern is a museum of international modern and contemporary art and is located in London. It is one of the most visited modern art galleries in the world. The art gallery itself is a converted power station and has several levels that hold permanent and temporary exhibits.

6. Van Gogh Museum

Vincent Van Gogh was one of the most influential artists of the 20th century. The Van Gogh Museum can be found in Amsterdam, the Netherlands. It holds the largest collection of Van Gogh paintings in the world and also showcases the works of many of his contemporaries. Here are a few highlights you can expect to see:

  • The Sunflowers
  • The Bedroom
  • Almond Blossom

7. Guggenheim Museum Bilbao

The Guggenheim Museum in Bilbao, Spain is a museum of modern and contemporary art. Inside the museum you’ll find a range of permanent and visiting exhibits by Spanish and international artists. Even the building itself is worth seeing. Designed by famed architect Frank Gehry, the futuristic looking building is definitely a sight worth seeing.

8. Vatican Museums

The Vatican Museums are world-renowned for their collections of medieval and Renaissance art. One of the biggest attractions is the Sistine Chapel, which is on the visitor route through the Vatican Museums. Be warned though, the entry queues can be notoriously long, so it's best to book your tickets in advance.

9. Galleria Degli Uffizi

The Uffizi Gallery is one of the oldest art museums in the world. It is located in Florence, Italy and is home to many famous artworks like the Birth of Venus by Botticelli. Since it’s such a popular tourist destination, queues to get into the gallery can become extremely long during peak seasons. Consider taking a summer holiday in Italy to explore more of what Tuscany has to offer.

10. Hermitage Museum

Russia might not be one of the most visited destinations in Europe, but if you ever make it to the city of St. Petersburg then the Hermitage Museum is worth a visit. It’s one of the oldest museums in the world and holds a large collection of art from around Europe. The scale of the collection is truly breath-taking, covering everything from prehistoric artifacts to post-impressionist masterpieces.

From London to Paris to St. Petersburg, there are just so many art galleries to choose from. I’ve been lucky enough to visit a few of these, however the rest are firmly on my must-see list. Have you ever visited any of these art galleries and museums? Which art galleries are on your must-see list?

Frequently Asked Questions

What are some top art galleries to visit in Europe?
Must-see galleries include the Louvre in Paris, the Uffizi in Florence, and the Tate Modern in London.
Are these galleries suitable for first-time visitors?
Yes, they offer guided tours and have accessible information perfect for beginners.
Can I buy art at these galleries?
Most galleries focus on exhibitions, but some have gift shops with prints and art-inspired items.
What is the best time to visit European art galleries?
Weekdays and early mornings are less crowded, especially outside of peak tourist seasons.
Are these galleries family-friendly?
Many galleries offer family programs and interactive exhibits to engage all ages.
Do I need to book tickets in advance?
Booking online ahead of time is recommended to avoid long lines, especially in popular galleries.
Are there special exhibits for women artists in these galleries?
Yes, some galleries feature exhibitions highlighting women artists and their contributions.
Is photography allowed inside these galleries?
Photography rules vary; some allow non-flash photos, but it's best to check each gallery's policy.
Are there guided tours focused on female artists?
Several galleries offer specialized tours spotlighting women artists and their history.
How can I combine visiting art galleries with other activities?
Many galleries are near cafes, shopping districts, and historic sites, making it easy to explore more in the area.
