# 10 Must\-See Art Galleries in Europe for Culture Lovers

The art galleries in Europe are some of the most visited in the world. There are many famous art galleries in Europe and most of them are popular tourist attractions. If you’re ever lucky enough to travel through Europe, a visit to any of the major art galleries will have you viewing masterpieces from throughout the ages. These are just a few of the must-see art galleries in Europe for every culture seeker.

## 1. Musée Du Louvre

[The Louvre](https://www.louvre.fr/en) is without a doubt one of the most popular art galleries in Europe, possibly even the world. It attracts more than 8 million visitors a year and is home to thousands of priceless paintings and antiquities, with the most famous being the portrait of the Mona Lisa. The Louvre is a Parisian icon and a must-see upon visiting the city. If you are planning a trip, don't forget to check out our [packing tips for a European vacation](https://travel.allwomenstalk.com/packing-tips-for-a-european-vacation/) to ensure you're prepared for long museum walks.

## 2. Museo Del Prado

[The Museo del Prado](https://www.museodelprado.es/en) is located in central Madrid, Spain. It is the main Spanish national art museum and holds one of the world’s finest collections of European art. Much of the collection comes from Spanish royalty and features many works by famous Spanish artists like Goya and Velázquez.

## 3. Musée National D'Art Moderne

If you prefer modern art then check out the [Musée National d'Art Moderne](https://www.centrepompidou.fr/en/the-centre-pompidou/the-musee-national-dart-moderne). It is located in Paris, France and is the largest modern art gallery in Europe. It also has the second largest collection of modern art in the world, making it a cornerstone for anyone interested in 20th-century movements.

## 4. Victoria and Albert Museum

[The Victoria and Albert Museum](https://www.vam.ac.uk/) is located in central London. It is the world’s largest museum of decorative arts and design. Inside the art gallery you’ll find art spanning 5000 years, from ancient times to the present day. The collections hold pieces from all over the world and cover everything from architecture, jewellery, fashion, photography, and sculpture. For more inspiration, see our guide on [free things to do in London](https://travel.allwomenstalk.com/fun-and-free-things-to-do-in-london/).

## 5. Tate Modern

[The Tate Modern](https://www.tate.org.uk/visit/tate-modern) is a museum of international modern and contemporary art and is located in London. It is one of the most visited modern art galleries in the world. The art gallery itself is a converted power station and has several levels that hold permanent and temporary exhibits.

## 6. Van Gogh Museum

Vincent Van Gogh was one of the most influential artists of the 20th century. [The Van Gogh Museum](https://www.vangoghmuseum.nl/en) can be found in Amsterdam, the Netherlands. It holds the largest collection of Van Gogh paintings in the world and also showcases the works of many of his contemporaries. Here are a few highlights you can expect to see:

- The Sunflowers

- The Bedroom

- Almond Blossom

## 7. Guggenheim Museum Bilbao

[The Guggenheim Museum](https://www.guggenheim-bilbao.eus/en) in Bilbao, Spain is a museum of modern and contemporary art. Inside the museum you’ll find a range of permanent and visiting exhibits by Spanish and international artists. Even the building itself is worth seeing. Designed by famed architect Frank Gehry, the futuristic looking building is definitely a sight worth seeing.

## 8. Vatican Museums

[The Vatican Museums](https://www.museivaticani.va/) are world-renowned for their collections of medieval and Renaissance art. One of the biggest attractions is the Sistine Chapel, which is on the visitor route through the Vatican Museums. Be warned though, the entry queues can be notoriously long, so it's best to book your tickets in advance.

## 9. Galleria Degli Uffizi

[The Uffizi Gallery](https://www.uffizi.it/en/the-uffizi) is one of the oldest art museums in the world. It is located in Florence, Italy and is home to many famous artworks like the Birth of Venus by Botticelli. Since it’s such a popular tourist destination, queues to get into the gallery can become extremely long during peak seasons. Consider taking a [summer holiday in Italy](https://travel.allwomenstalk.com/gorgeous-destinations-for-a-summer-holiday-in-italy/) to explore more of what Tuscany has to offer.

## 10. Hermitage Museum

Russia might not be one of the most visited destinations in Europe, but if you ever make it to the city of St. Petersburg then the [Hermitage Museum](https://www.hermitagemuseum.org/) is worth a visit. It’s one of the oldest museums in the world and holds a large collection of art from around Europe. The scale of the collection is truly breath-taking, covering everything from prehistoric artifacts to post-impressionist masterpieces.
