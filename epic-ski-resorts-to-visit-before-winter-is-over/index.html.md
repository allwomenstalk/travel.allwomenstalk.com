# 7 Epic Ski Resorts to Visit before Winter is over ...

There are so many ski resorts to visit, so it can be hard to choose which one to try next. Not only is skiing super fun, but it’s also a great way to burn calories and work your muscles. Trying out new ski resorts whenever you can is a great way to keep things fresh and exciting while also giving you a chance to see some breathtaking scenery. So, if you’re on the lookout for ski resorts to visit, check out this list.

## 1. Breckenridge, Colorado

![Breckenridge, Colorado](https://resize.allw.mn/1028x0/filters:format(webp)/filters:quality(70)/content/ob/ms/t9/dv1zi866_600x400.jpg)

I’m from Colorado, so I can attest to the fact that Breckenridge is one of the best ski resorts to visit in all of America. Not only is there great skiing, but the town also has plenty to offer in the way of lodging and eating when you finish your last run for the day. The summit here is nearly 13,000 feet so there are a huge variety of runs for skill levels of all kinds. With plenty of new snow throughout the winter, skiing here is always epic.

## 2. Mammoth Mountain, California

![Mammoth Mountain, California](https://resize.allw.mn/1028x0/filters:format(webp)/filters:quality(70)/content/mq/sd/uy/mdum2k50_600x375.jpg)

California isn’t all sun and beaches. There are lots of great places to ski and Mammoth Mountain is one of the best. The place gets over 400 inches of perfect powder every year and hosts more than one million visitors during each ski season. On top of that, it boasts an outstanding terrain park for the daredevil in all of us.

## 3. Jackson Hole, Wyoming

![Jackson Hole, Wyoming](https://resize.allw.mn/1028x0/filters:format(webp)/filters:quality(70)/content/hf/wa/wx/07jfqmdq_600x448.jpg)

Jackson Hole is a ski resort that most avid skiers have visited and have an opinion about. Most are good reports, so that makes this a definite must to add to your list. With almost 500 inches of snow every year and 2,500 skiable acres, this ski resort is loaded with plenty of opportunities to enjoy the winter, no matter if you’re a beginner or an expert. The town nearby is full of great restaurants and nightlife so grab a room and stay for a while.

## 4. Taos Ski Valley, New Mexico

![Taos Ski Valley, New Mexico](https://resize.allw.mn/1028x0/filters:format(webp)/filters:quality(70)/content/ng/er/xs/pbf0kd8o_600x351.jpg)

If you think all of New Mexico is hot, arid desert, you’re really missing out on some great skiing. Located in the Sangre de Cristo Mountains, Taos Ski Valley boasts a nearly 13,000-foot peak, well-maintained trails and plenty of sunshine. Taos is a funky little town, so there’s lots to do when you leave the slopes too. This place boasts a huge number of expert trails, so check it out if you need a new challenge.

## 5. Park City, Utah

![Park City, Utah](https://resize.allw.mn/1028x0/filters:format(webp)/filters:quality(70)/content/ac/dd/fe/brvoxqpz_600x403.jpg)

No list would be complete without including Park City. It’s won an award for the best family ski resort, so it’s definitely worth a visit if you have a house full of tiny, budding skiers. There’s an expert ski school, a 4,000-foot alpine coaster and a night terrain park. There’s definitely something for everyone here, so put it on your next vacation agenda.

## 6. Okemo Mountain Resort, Vermont

![Okemo Mountain Resort, Vermont](https://resize.allw.mn/1028x0/filters:format(webp)/filters:quality(70)/content/fq/io/h8/zucvnbvu_600x400.jpg)

Vermont is a great place to ski – it gets plenty of snow in the winter. Okemo Mountain boasts over 100 trails and makes snow when needed so you’ll always find the most optimal conditions, no matter when you hit the slopes. This place is ranked in the top 20 Eastern snow resorts, so it’s a definite must.

## 7. Holiday Valley Resort, New York

![Holiday Valley Resort, New York](https://resize.allw.mn/1028x0/filters:format(webp)/filters:quality(70)/content/a6/iy/da/dv1z81dg_600x450.jpg)

New York is more than the Statue of Liberty and Times Square. Check out the Holiday Valley Resort for an experience you’ll never forget. It’s smaller than the average ski resort, so it’s naturally not as crowded or touristy. The new ski lodge is perfect for getting something to eat or drink or just to take a break.

What’s your favorite ski resort? Do you like to go to the same one or try new ones all the time?
