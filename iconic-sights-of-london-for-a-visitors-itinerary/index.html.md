# 43 Iconic Sights of London for a Visitor's Itinerary ...

There are so many iconic sights in London that you could easily spend two weeks in this fabulous city and still not see everything you want to see. The iconic sights of London are known worldwide and for jolly good reason.

## 1. Leadenhall Market

![Leadenhall Market](https://resize.allw.mn/1028x0/filters:format(webp)/filters:quality(70)/content/af/p7/gdfmtv9s_427x640.jpg)

Via [Leadenhall Market, London](https://www.flickr.com/photos/craig_r1978/3152726007/in/set-1606948/)

The city's markets are among the great attractions of London. The markets are extremely numerous and are all around the city. Most are known for their one outstandingly unique feature - either for its location or the goods it sells. Leadenhall is a beautiful cobblestoned market right in the heart of the city. If you've an eagle eye, you will spot Leadenhall market as being one of the filming locations for Diagon Alley.

## 2. Guards at Buckingham Palace

![Guards at Buckingham Palace](https://resize.allw.mn/1028x0/filters:format(webp)/filters:quality(70)/content/ri/iu/fz36r39y_427x640.jpg)

Via [Old Guard March out of ...](https://www.flickr.com/photos/bjcc/6217440429/in/pool-51195860@N00/)

You don't have to wait for special occasions to experience the pomp and circumstance of London's traditions. The Changing of the Guard at Buckingham Palace takes place daily.

## 3. Big Ben

![Big Ben](https://resize.allw.mn/1028x0/filters:format(webp)/filters:quality(70)/content/ek/hk/hsp6bpv7_584x877.jpg)

Via [Things That Amaze Me](https://www.pinterest.com/pin/498844096196073713/)

Big Ben in all its glory and one of the most iconic sights of London. Did you know the tower is actually The Elizabeth Tower - renamed from St. Stephen's Tower in honor of Her Majesty's Diamond Jubilee in 2012. Big Ben is actually the bell in the tower.

## 4. Buckingham Palace

![Buckingham Palace](https://resize.allw.mn/1028x0/filters:format(webp)/filters:quality(70)/content/ot/r5/gww8hyep_483x640.jpg)

Via [Buckingham Palace, London, England](https://www.flickr.com/photos/richlewis/3103751851/in/photostream/)

The gates open onto the most famous residence in the world, alongside the American White House. Oh what history those gates have opened onto.

## 5. Westminster Abbey

![Westminster Abbey](https://resize.allw.mn/1028x0/filters:format(webp)/filters:quality(70)/content/jq/b2/lxjcb6ma_455x640.jpg)

Via [Westminster Abby, London England](https://www.flickr.com/photos/theblu26/5141942323/)

As well as being a normal functioning church, the abbey is the home of practically every coronation of the King/Queen of England (and later Britain) since William the Conqueror. It is the burial place of most monarchs and also the site of the majority of the royal weddings.

## 6. Tower Bridge

![Tower Bridge](https://resize.allw.mn/1028x0/filters:format(webp)/filters:quality(70)/content/c0/qh/wq81vzu0_397x600.jpg)

Via [Tower Bridge at Dusk](https://www.artflakes.com/en/products/tower-bridge-at-dusk)

This is the bridge that has to open to allow tall ships to pass through.

## 7. Liberty & Co

![Liberty & Co](https://resize.allw.mn/1028x0/filters:format(webp)/filters:quality(70)/content/dm/e1/iceys1xh_400x700.jpg)

Via [Liberty & Co. Store on ...](https://www.flickr.com/photos/momentaryshutter/5046119143/)

Liberty has anchored Regent Street since 1875. The luxury goods department store is instantly recognizable for its Tudor frontage.

## 8. Camden Market

![Camden Market](https://resize.allw.mn/1028x0/filters:format(webp)/filters:quality(70)/content/sp/lq/zfja220w_480x640.jpg)

Via [Camden Lock Market 027](https://www.flickr.com/photos/36223620@N05/6925797752/in/set-72157629438653772/)

Camden Market is one of the most eclectic markets in London. It is spread out across Camden Lock and in old buildings and is a definite shopping destination not to be missed.

## 9. Isabella Plantation, Richmond Park

![Isabella Plantation, Richmond Park](https://resize.allw.mn/1028x0/filters:format(webp)/filters:quality(70)/content/fh/tc/n1i1cykr_600x900.jpg)

Via [All sizes | Isabella Plantation, ...](https://www.flickr.com/photos/14463685@N07/7190295888/sizes/l/)

Richmond Park is one of the city's eight Royal Parks and the largest. It is a conservation area and is known for its herd of deer as well as its stunning natural beauty. There's a fantastic view of the city from King Henry's Mound.

## 10. Peter Pan Statue

![Peter Pan Statue](https://resize.allw.mn/1028x0/filters:format(webp)/filters:quality(70)/content/w4/rx/ub8y024u_600x600.jpg)

Via [The boy is back in ...](https://www.dailymail.co.uk/tvshowbiz/article-1186203/The-boy-town--Peter-Pan-returns-dazzling-new-singing-dancing-high-tech-production.html)

London is packed with statues. There are great figures from British history and there are fictional figures that have become household names - such as Peter Pan, which stands in Kensington Gardens.

## 11. 10 Downing Street

![10 Downing Street](https://resize.allw.mn/1028x0/filters:format(webp)/filters:quality(70)/content/i4/vo/grlbqhge_300x600.jpg)

Via [baenk.com](https://goo.gl/wOS9sr)

The most famous front door in the World. Home of the UK Prime Minister.

## 12. The Thames

_ Via Worldwide Tour & Travel - ..._

The great river is the lifeblood of London.

## 13. Palace of Westminster

![Palace of Westminster](https://resize.allw.mn/1028x0/filters:format(webp)/filters:quality(70)/content/k3/wm/v9ts2iml_416x640.jpg)

Via [Westminster Spikes & Shadows](https://www.flickr.com/photos/pepeketua/3553365707/)

Many of the places to visit in London are wonderful Gothic buildings, including The Palace of Westminster - more commonly known as the Houses of Parliament.

## 14. Soho

![Soho](https://resize.allw.mn/1028x0/filters:format(webp)/filters:quality(70)/content/bu/r5/mrvtqiuo_517x776.jpg)

Via [Le pouce-pouce de Soho](https://www.flickr.com/photos/pixom/11550596555/in/set-72157639016953004)

By day or night, there's always something happening in Soho. This West End district is home to Theatreland, Piccadilly Circus and tons of nightclubs and restaurants.

## 15. Trafalgar Square

![Trafalgar Square](https://resize.allw.mn/1028x0/filters:format(webp)/filters:quality(70)/content/bh/vi/o2xw3k89_434x640.jpg)

Via [London. Trafalgar Square. Londres](https://www.flickr.com/photos/alcaide/5462329737/)

Trafalgar Square is one of the unmissable places to visit in London. It's hugely photogenic with its Lions by Edward Landseer, Nelson's Column and its fountains. Thousands gather here to see in the New Year.

## 16. Piccadilly Circus

![Piccadilly Circus](https://resize.allw.mn/1028x0/filters:format(webp)/filters:quality(70)/content/m5/bl/bgg5uhl9_569x1002.jpg)

Via [Piccadilly Shops, Department Stores and ...](https://goo.gl/0F3Evc)

The bright lights of the West End and let's not forgot the statue of Eros in the center, which is not seen in this photograph.

## 17. London Black Cab

![London Black Cab](https://resize.allw.mn/1028x0/filters:format(webp)/filters:quality(70)/content/l5/pm/eo9u1oto_600x750.jpg)

Via [London Photography, fine art photograph ...](https://www.etsy.com?ref=allwomenstalk.com)

Surely one of the most recognizable vehicles in the world.

## 18. London Eye

![London Eye](https://resize.allw.mn/1028x0/filters:format(webp)/filters:quality(70)/content/is/p0/ry4dfwn6_426x640.jpg)

Via [All sizes | London Eye ...](https://www.flickr.com/photos/jpn/4718454145/sizes/l/in/photostream/)

Since 2000, the London Eye (originally known as the Millennium Wheel) has been one of the newer iconic sights of London. It offers fabulous views across the city.

## 19. The Sherlock Holmes Museum

![The Sherlock Holmes Museum](https://resize.allw.mn/1028x0/filters:format(webp)/filters:quality(70)/content/p2/w2/lk7yfxrn_399x640.jpg)

Via [London 18-08-2012](https://www.flickr.com/photos/karen_roe/7980093147/)

As well as some of the most amazing museums in the World, there are lots of little boutique museums in the city.

## 20. Queen's Walk

![Queen's Walk](https://resize.allw.mn/1028x0/filters:format(webp)/filters:quality(70)/content/wc/er/j1plu9l0_481x894.jpg)

Via [Top 10 Most Magnificent Perspectives ...](https://www.topinspired.com/most-magnificent-perspectives-of-london/)

There are various areas along the north and south embankments for a stroll, all offering scenic views.

## 21. The Reading Room, British Museum

![The Reading Room, British Museum](https://resize.allw.mn/1028x0/filters:format(webp)/filters:quality(70)/content/uv/xo/pe28ob2r_400x600.jpg)

buildings.

![The Natural History Museum](https://resize.allw.mn/1028x0/filters:format(webp)/filters:quality(70)/content/gd/bc/c6ir0ykp_427x640.jpg)

Via [st pauls and rain](https://www.flickr.com/photos/stickygum/7062463775/)

## 30. Tower of London

![Tower of London](https://resize.allw.mn/1028x0/filters:format(webp)/filters:quality(70)/content/n3/mt/mtgeotgq_425x640.jpg)

Via [Jewel house](https://www.flickr.com/photos/mollow/2684343713/)

the Tower of London - aka The White Tower - must have the bloodiest history of any building in the UK. Today there are no prisoners and no beheadings. There are ravens, Beefeater (Yeomen of the Guard), few ghosts and the Crown Jewels, which are kept in the Jewel House pictured above.

## 31. Oxford Street

![Oxford Street](https://resize.allw.mn/1028x0/filters:format(webp)/filters:quality(70)/content/x0/mi/vapj286g_331x498.jpg)

Via [Close your eyes and dream ...](https://enchantedengland.tumblr.com/post/14640159056/oxford-street-in-london-england-at)

London's (and probably the UK's) première high street.

## 32. Hyde Park

![Hyde Park](https://resize.allw.mn/1028x0/filters:format(webp)/filters:quality(70)/content/yv/nx/r0a55bke_472x700.jpg)

Via [My Fotolog](https://myfotolog.tumblr.com/post/15509000450/hyde-park-london)

A huge green space in the city. Home to sporting events, concerts and festivals, Speaker's Corner, the Serpentine Lake and the Serpentine Art Gallery.

## 33. Royal Albert Hall

![Royal Albert Hall](https://resize.allw.mn/1028x0/filters:format(webp)/filters:quality(70)/content/ca/cj/bz66jmxl_424x640.jpg)

## 34. Neal's Yard

Tucked away just up the road from Covent Garden tube station, Neal's Yard is a darling little alley/square of boutique shops - mostly "new age" in feel.

![Marble Arch](https://resize.allw.mn/1028x0/filters:format(webp)/filters:quality(70)/content/qr/vq/coj7s4f7_600x450.jpg)

## 40. Greenwich Observatory

_ Via The British Traveler_

Home to the clock (On Shepherd's Gate) that signifies GMT.

## 41. HMS-Belfast

![HMS-Belfast](https://resize.allw.mn/1028x0/filters:format(webp)/filters:quality(70)/content/md/ji/mwkujo6h_600x450.jpg)

Via [Historic ships | Exploring London](https://exploring-london.com/category/historic-ships/)

The ship is permanently moored on the Thames near Tower Bridge and is now a museum. It serves a reminder of British naval power.

## 42. Covent Garden

![Covent Garden](https://resize.allw.mn/1028x0/filters:format(webp)/filters:quality(70)/content/un/eb/ehmj22eu_427x640.jpg)

Via [Covent Garden](https://www.flickr.com/photos/ms_abitibi/9464499106/)

Once the city's central produce and flower market, Covent Garden is now one of the great tourist destinations in the city. The piazza is lined with restaurants and there's usually plenty of street entertainment to be enjoyed after you've browsed the shops in the covered walkways and the craft market.
