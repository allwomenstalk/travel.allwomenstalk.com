# 7 Tell\-Tale Signs You Know You're From Pennsylvania When...

Growing up in the Keystone State, you don't realize how unique your habits are until you leave. When I moved away for college, the "you know you're from Pennsylvania when" moments started creeping into every conversation. To me, certain phrases and traditions were just normal. It wasn't until I talked with friends in New England that I realized how distinctly Pennsylvanian I really was. Here are the clear signs you're from PA.

## 1 PA

The biggest sign you’re from Pennsylvania is by saying you’re from PA. If you've spent time [living in the Northeast](https://travel.allwomenstalk.com/northeast-travel-guide/), you'll notice that most people don't use their state's abbreviation in casual speech. You don’t hear people say, “Yeah, I’m from TX” or “That’s right, I’m from RI.” Pennsylvanians are the only people who use their abbreviation. And we can’t claim it’s because of length because California and South Carolina have the same number of syllables, yet we’re the only ones sticking to the initials.

## 2 “Down the Shore”

Why do people say that you go “down the shore” instead of “to the beach?” It’s a quirk mainly tailored to the eastern part of PA which is close to [New Jersey’s beautiful beaches](https://www.visitnj.org/new-jersey-beaches). Interestingly, the beach we commonly go to isn’t even south of us! We don’t say we’re “going down to D.C.” which is indeed south of us. This one makes the list for sure! If you're heading there, you might want to check out the [Wawa](#5) run requirements first.

## 3 Hunting Season

The first day of hunting season is a school holiday. That means, you don’t have school. My friends in Vermont were baffled by this because not everybody hunts. So why should they have the day off? Well, there haven’t been any complaints from the kids so that solves that.

- Schools close across the state for the opener.
- Local businesses often see a dip in morning foot traffic.
- Hunter orange becomes the unofficial state color of the week.

## 4 Fire Hall Wedding Reception

Another sign you’re from the Keystone State is your casual reaction to the phrase “fire hall wedding reception.” I’ve been to wedding receptions, baby showers, and parties at fire halls. If you're [planning a unique wedding](https://weddings.allwomenstalk.com/unique-wedding-venues/), you've likely considered them as they are easy to rent out and clean up isn’t bad at all. But if you think about it, it sounds like you’re bonkers to outsiders! My friends did a double-take when I told them, unwilling to believe that it’s really a thing.

## 5 Wawa

Okay, okay, I know Wawa can be found in more than one state, but still. The addiction of Wawa is not universal. How, I can’t tell you. [Wawa](https://www.wawa.com/) is an up-scale convenience store attached to a gas station, though they can stand on their own. My sister goes to school in Maryland and even there, they don’t seem to have the same fixation. In PA, it’s completely normal to go there for gas, food or just because, multiple times a day. After all, you can find one about every ten minutes along the road. In Vermont, they sure didn’t know about Wawa; a loss in my opinion.

| Feature | Eastern PA (Philly Area) | Western PA (Pittsburgh Area) |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Go-To Spot | Wawa | Sheetz |
| The Beach | "The Shore" | Lake Erie or MD/DE |
| Local Slang | "Youse" | "Yinz" |

Compare this obsession with our [shore trips](#2).

## 6 Electric Candles

Electric candles are always in the windows. Driving through a neighborhood you see house after house lit up. But did you know these candles are a sign of hospitality? Originally they were used during the Civil War to guide individuals on the [Underground Railroad](https://www.nps.gov/subjects/undergroundrailroad/index.htm). They meant that the house with the lights would help in any way they could. Now, they are just used for decoration and a warm sign of welcome.

## 7 Town Names

We’ve got quite the pick of town names. Virginville, Intercourse, Climax and Paradise are all Pennsylvania towns. What’s more, you probably know how to pronounce the names “Wilkes-Barre,” “Bryn Mawr,” “Conshohocken,” “Knoebles,” and “Schuylkill.” If you’re looking for entertainment, try to get your out-of-state friends to pronounce these [hard to pronounce PA towns](https://travel.allwomenstalk.com/weirdest-town-names-in-america/). You can’t help but laugh while they stumble over the words!

These are just a few tell-tale signs you know you’re from PA. They may sound sane to you, but that’s the first sign that you’ve been infected. What are other signs that show you’re from Pennsylvania? Is there one you really related to?
