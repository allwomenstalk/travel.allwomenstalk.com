Worldwide Adventures: a Bucket List of Unpredictable and Non-Clich Destinations ...

By Neecey

Paris, London, Rome, New York, Rio de Janeiro, Tokyo … they head up every travel bucket list going. But what if you have been to those places? Or, what if you don’t actually even want to go to these places? As fantastic as these places are, there are so many other destinations around the world that are worthy of a slot on your bucket list. Here are those places:

1. Yerevan, Armenia

The Armenian capital is overlooked by Mount Ararat and the city is filled with amazing architecture. Its people welcome you with a huge, friendly hug.

2. New Orleans, USA

Get your jazz on. A multi-cultural and most happening city. Must see Vieux Carré (French Quarter) and Bourbon Street.

3. Reykjavik, Iceland

One of the world's most artistic cities. Spend an evening in a pub where locals entertain with stories and songs.

4. Killarney, Ireland

See authentic Ireland. Nature, history and culture combine to give Killarney a special energy.

5. Isle of Skye, Scotland

One of the most stunning islands of the UK. Must eat fish and chips in Portree.

6. Galle, Sri Lanka

Beaches, fabulous food and friendly folks - what's not to love? A distinctly colonial feel and a must see is the Portuguese fort.

7. Lofoten Islands, Norway

One of the most stunningly beautiful places on Earth. Hike, swim, just stand and stare.

8. Okinawa, Japan

Find the secret of long life in the history and culture on a gorgeous island.

9. Montreal, Canada

Cosmopolitan and urbane with a distinctly European flavor.

10. Mestia, Georgia

Tucked away in an isolated corner of the Caucasus Mountains, this is the place to mingle with the locals. Must drink Georgian wine and hike to a glacier.

11. Beirut, Lebanon

Often called the Paris of the Middle East, this is a great place to come and party.

12. Gallipoli, Turkey

An amazing site of history (one of the bloodiest battles of World War I), and a gorgeous location on the Aegean Sea.

13. Big Sur, California

A world away from the huge metropolises of California. It's the place to soak in views and hear the crash of waves on cliffs.

14. Freetown Christiana, Denmark

Indulge your inner hippy in the artsy, free-governing city within a city (Christiania is part of Copenhagen).

15. Juqueí, Brazil

As well as beautiful beaches, this is seafood heaven, Foodies take note,

16. Varanasi, India

One of the oldest continuously inhabited cities on Earth. Visit here and experience the real soul of India.

17. Broome, Australia

Yep, you really can ride a camel on a stunning beach in Australia.

18. Zaanse Schans, the Netherlands

An easy trip from Amsterdam, this is the land of Dutch windmills and dykes.

19. Tulum, Mexico

A magnificent mix of history and beaches. Mayan ruins right next to superb stretches of sand.

20. Plitvice Lakes National Park, Croatia

This UNESCO site is one of the most magnificent natural wonders of Europe. Discover lakes, waterfalls and caves, and a host of wildlife.

21. Cartagena, Columbia

This colonial gem will charm you with its architecture, history and beaches.

22. Taman Negara National Park, Malaysia

Walk a tree canopy through the jungle and see if you can spot some of the world's most endangered species of tigers, rhinos, leopards and Asian elephants.

23. Belgrade, Serbia

A pretty much undiscovered gem among Europe's capital cities.

24. Inle Lake, Myanmar

Discover a simple way of life among the humble people of the beautiful country around Lake Inle.

25. Gothenburg, Sweden

Known for its artistic and creative side, Gothenburg is one of the friendliest cities in Europe.

26. Isla De Vieques, Puerto Rico

Sometimes, all you need is crystalline waters and pristine sandy beaches.

27. Rumbur Valley, Pakistan

A real opportunity to escape the modern world and live off the grid without electricity or cell phone signals.

28. Ljubljana, Slovenia

A capital city with plenty to explore. Great nightlife too.

29. Eilat, Israel

Superb resorts and a fabulous place to learn to scuba dive.

30. El Chaltén, Argentina

This village with a fab hippy vibe may be small but it sits among some of the most magnificent mountain scenery in the world.

31. Bruges, Belgium

Discover canals, lace, beer and chocolate in a magnificent medieval setting.

32. Knysna, South Africa

Use as a base to discover South Africa's very special Garden Route.

33. Monasterio De Piedra Park, Spain

Love history? Love hiking? You'll love this scenic waterfall park that includes the site of a Moorish monastery.

34. Ulan Bator, Mongolia

One of the remotest destinations of the planet but the journey and the effort is so worth it. It oozes history and culture with masses to see.

35. White Sands, New Mexico, USA

One of the world's most unique landscapes. The gypsum (white sand) dunes cover an area of 275 square miles.

36. Rotorua, New Zealand

Scenic and relaxing, the mud pools and geysers are a world away from the adrenaline and adventure capital of Queenstown.

37. Kazan, Russia

Kazan presents a wonderful combination of Christianity and Islam and a palette of Europe and Asia.

38. Corsica, France

An incredibly beautiful Mediterranean island with a fabulous coastline and scenic and atmospheric interior.

39. Jiuzhaigou Valley Scenic and Historic Interest Area, China

The UNESCO Heritage Listed park is home to some of the most photogenic waterfalls in the world.

40. Chavin De Huantar, Peru

Slow down a pace and explore the Inca site which is centuries older than Machu Picchu.

41. Lake Garda, Italy

Italy's lake country is the perfect antidote to the cares of the world.

42. Isfahan, Iran

Some of the most magnificent examples of Islamic architecture you'll find anywhere.

43. Ha Long Bay, Vietnam

Mysterious and magical, a place to rejuvenate the soul.

44. Budva, Montenegro

One of the smallest countries in Europe has a big capacity to party. Budva is a charming old town perfect for the beach by day, bars by night.

45. Atacama Desert, Chile

Hike by day and be in awe of the amazing scenery and then at night turn your eyes skyward for some stargazing.

46. Zanzibar, Tanzania

The picture says it all - a tropical paradise.

47. Seoraksan National Park, South Korea

Find peace with yourself and the world in the tranquility of the mountains.

48. Bhutan

There's something to be said for a country that measures its economy on a happiness quota rather than dollars.

49. Freycinet National Park, Tasmania, Australia

Wineglass Bay will seduce into wanting to stay in Tasmania forever

50. Isla Margarita, Venezuela

One of the lesser-known Caribbean destinations but there's nothing underwhelming about this gorgeous place.

Frequently Asked Questions

What makes a destination non-cliché for travel?
Non-cliché destinations are unique, less crowded places offering authentic cultural experiences beyond typical tourist spots.
How can I find unpredictable destinations for my travel bucket list?
Explore local blogs, travel forums, and social media groups focused on off-the-beaten-path adventures and hidden gems.
Are these destinations safe for solo women travelers?
Many non-cliché spots are safe when you take standard precautions; research local customs and stay aware of your surroundings.
What are some must-pack essentials for unpredictable trips?
Pack versatile clothing, a reliable map or GPS, a first-aid kit, and portable phone chargers to handle unexpected situations.
How do I manage language barriers in less touristy places?
Learn basic local phrases, use translation apps, and consider hiring local guides for smoother communication.
Can these destinations fit into short vacation plans?
Yes, many unconventional places offer compact, rich experiences perfect for weekend or week-long trips.
What’s the best way to respect local cultures and communities?
Engage respectfully, support local businesses, and avoid behaviors that may offend or disrupt local traditions.
How can I budget for unpredictable travel adventures?
Plan flexible budgets with buffer funds for unexpected expenses, and choose affordable accommodations like guesthouses or homestays.
Are non-cliché destinations suitable for traveling with friends?
Absolutely! These unique spots often provide memorable group experiences away from typical tourist crowds.
How do I document and share my experiences from unique destinations?
Use journals, photos, and social media stories to capture and share your authentic travel moments with friends and followers.
