# 10 Really Weird Bars around the World ...

Weird bars are probably not the ones you would necessarily call your local. Most people consider a bar to be much of the same all around the world; beer on tap, greasy blotting paper food, loads of people, and a festive atmosphere. However, strange bars are everywhere, and drinking in one of these weird bars, will certainly be a night to remember. Here are 10 Really Weird Bars Around the World.

## 1. The Clinic, Singapore

![The Clinic, Singapore](https://resize.allw.mn/1028x0/filters:format(webp)/filters:quality(70)/content/travel/2012/06/1_the-clinic-singapore_600x398.jpg)

Exactly as the name describes, the Clinic is a hospital themed pub, fully kitted out with wheelchairs for chairs and steel operating tables for well, tables. If you order food, it will be served in a steel kidney shaped bowl. Order your drinks and sip from IV bags and test tubes. If you are feeling a little full after dinner, you can lie down on a white hospital bed and draw the hospital curtains closed around you until you regain your energy, and feel like going to boogie on the dance floor in the nightclub called Morphine.

## 2. The Icebar, Sweden

![The Icebar, Sweden](https://resize.allw.mn/1028x0/filters:format(webp)/filters:quality(70)/content/travel/2012/06/2_the-icebar-sweden_600x450.jpg)

Nestled right inside the Ice Hotel (yes really) in the small town of Jukkasjarvi, in Sweden, is one of the most acclaimed weird bars, the orginal Absolut Ice Bar. With everything made entirely from ice, don’t expect that you will be staying long, unless the drinks do the job of warming you up from the inside out. Even the glasses are made of ice. Sip your vodka cocktail out of an ice cocktail glass and try and not get it stuck to your lips, or your gloves which you will need to be wearing. Open during the winter months, you can really enjoy your chilly experience, just dress very warmly.

## 3. The Red Sea Star Underwater Bar, Restaurant and Observatory –Israel

![The Red Sea Star Underwater Bar, Restaurant and Observatory –Israel](https://resize.allw.mn/1028x0/filters:format(webp)/filters:quality(70)/content/travel/2012/06/3_the-red-sea-star-underwater-bar-restaurant-and-observatory-israel_560x390.jpg)

Now if there was ever a completely unique bar to visit, this must be it. Watch the marine life float by you as you sit comfortably, cocktail in hand no less than 6 metres under the sea. The vibrant decor shaped and colored like the bright coral reef around you, comes complete with sea anemone shaped seats and sea urchin cushions. With strong plexiglass keeping you dry, from either side of the restaurant and above, you can enjoy an unparalleled view of the sea around you.

## 4. Bojangles, Australia

![Bojangles, Australia](https://resize.allw.mn/1028x0/filters:format(webp)/filters:quality(70)/content/travel/2012/06/4_bojangles-australia_600x450.jpg)

Nothing says quirky like this weird bar in Alice Springs right smack in the middle of Australia. Everybody travelling through the red centre of Oz, on their way to Uluru, needs to stop in and pay a visit to this peculiar little establishment. The attention to detail may not be so apparent after a few cold ones, so be sure to take the full tour before you get down to any serious drinking. With the only rules being that there are absolutely no rules, you can be 100% sure that the later it gets here, the more interesting it is going to get. Look around and you will see snakes in tanks, boots hanging from the ceiling and even a coffin with a suit of armour inside with a sign that says ‘’How much to touch Ned’s nuts?” If you go to the bathroom and wash your hands, you could be in for a shock; each tap opens the water in another sink.

## 5. DM Bar, Estonia

![DM Bar, Estonia](https://resize.allw.mn/1028x0/filters:format(webp)/filters:quality(70)/content/travel/2012/06/5_dm-bar-estonia_600x450.jpg)

DM stands for Depeche Mode, yes really, and this oddity among unique bars is a shrine totally dedicated to the band, Depeche Mode. Located in the capital city of Tallinn in Estonia, this unusual dedication is massively popular with the locals. The walls are covered with memorabilia and the pop band from the UK is splashed all over the walls, with records, pictures, signed photographs, posters and just about any branding from the band mounted on every surface. All the songs on the play list are exclusively from Depeche Mode, of course that goes without saying. Even 2 band members from Depeche Mode, Martin Gore and Andy Fletcher, have spent an evening to remember at the bar, partying the night away.

## 6. Baobab Bar, South Africa

![Baobab Bar, South Africa](https://resize.allw.mn/1028x0/filters:format(webp)/filters:quality(70)/content/travel/2012/06/6_baobab-bar-south-africa_600x400.jpg)

Make no mistake, the South Africans know how to throw a party, and in true South African style they decided to have a few parties inside a tree. Not the most run of the mill place to open a bar, but when the van Heerdens discovered this 6000 year old Baobab tree, on their farm in Limpopo Province, they decided, why the hell not. 75m high, the inside of the quirky bar can take up to 40 people inside, and the natural ventilation keeps the heat from the Highveld away from your beer. Originally opened for the thirsty locals, tourists are coming from all over the world to enjoy a frosty one here inside a tree.

## 7. Skeleton Bar – Switzerland

![Skeleton Bar – Switzerland](https://resize.allw.mn/1028x0/filters:format(webp)/filters:quality(70)/content/travel/2012/06/7_skeleton-bar-switzerland_600x450.jpg)

Not for the faint hearted, this horror movie set slash museum slash really bizarre bar, is beyond peculiar, but very different. Designed by the set designer for the Alien movies (need we say more?), the Swiss Oscar winning designer, Hans Rudi Giger, has decorated the entire drinking hole with bones from skeletons. Sit on skeleton bone chairs, lean up against bone walls, and enjoy the flowers on your table in the skeleton vases. If you are visiting Gruyere in Switzerland, and you have the stomach for this one, they serve alcohol so that might help; this is one stop you have to make!

## 8. Floyd’s Pelican Bar, Jamaica

![Floyd’s Pelican Bar, Jamaica](https://resize.allw.mn/1028x0/filters:format(webp)/filters:quality(70)/content/travel/2012/06/8_floyds-pelican-bar-jamaica_600x373.jpg)

Like something out of a Caribbean dream, this apparition off Parottee Bay is just a mile off the coastline of South Jamaica. Without looking twice, you may be forgiven for thinking that that small smudge on the edge of the skyline is nothing more than a dot in the ocean, but don’t be fooled for long. Get a local fisherman to take you down there, and as you get closer you will see the coolest place in all of the Caribbean, made entirely out of old palm leaves and some driftwood. Carve your name in the floor, snorkel around before lunch, and enjoy fresh lobster made by none other than Floyd himself. Wash it down with an ice cold beer and paradise is made.

## 9. Alux Restaurant and Lounge, Mexico

![Alux Restaurant and Lounge, Mexico](https://resize.allw.mn/1028x0/filters:format(webp)/filters:quality(70)/content/travel/2012/06/9_alux-restaurant-and-lounge-mexico_600x399.jpg)

Right in the middle of Playa Del Carmen, you can sit down and enjoy a sublime culinary treat, right inside a real cavern. Float between the various chambers with your drink in hand, with a back ground of charismatic lighting bringing the stalagmites and stalactites to life in blue, pink and purple. As one of only two places in the world that you can enjoy an evening meal and a good bottle of wine inside a cave no less, this strange bar and restaurant should be the first on your list when you arrive in Playa Del Carmen.

## 10. William Thornton Floating Bar and Restaurant, British Virgin Islands

![William Thornton Floating Bar and Restaurant, British Virgin Islands](https://resize.allw.mn/1028x0/filters:format(webp)/filters:quality(70)/content/travel/2012/06/10_william-thornton-floating-bar-and-restaurant-british-virgin-islands_600x450.jpg)

The Willy T, as it is affectionately known, belongs to intrepid owners, Annie and Mick Gardner, who have had the charming boat way back since the 80’s. Originally an authentic wooden Baltic vessel, it started to leak and sank right where it was anchored. Now proudly restored to a sturdy steel version of itself, the 100 foot schooner is moored just off Normal Island and is complete with a full bar and restaurant.

Weird bars make a night out something completely different and depending what your tastes are, you will most certainly be able to find a strange bar to drink in, wherever you may be. These 10 Really Weird Bars Around the World are something completely out of the ordinary. Which one of the strange pubs on this list would you go to?
