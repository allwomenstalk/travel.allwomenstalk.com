10 Ways to Volunteer in Africa ...

Reef Doctor – Antanarivo, Madagascar • Gap Year for Grown Ups • Jinja, Uganda • Cross-Cultural Solutions • The Rhythm of West Africa Semester • More ...

By Neecey

If you love to travel to fascinating places but also want to do something to give back, there are some brilliant ways to volunteer in Africa. The African continent is astounding, breathtakingly and stunningly beautiful but also wild and unforgiving. Life is hard and challenging in many areas with millions still living in abject poverty, and resources are inadequate, overstretched and unevenly distributed. There is truly an amazing diversity in the ways to volunteer in Africa and you don’t need any special skills to make a difference.

1. Reef Doctor – Antanarivo, Madagascar

One of the best ways to volunteer in Africa is to get involved with looking after the environment. Madagascar is an extremely poor country, but with some of the most spectacular dive sites in the world. Reef Doctor offers you free dive training as well as certification and real time marine research experience. You also get to work with the small local fisherman and their communities, while exploring one of the largest reefs in the world.
reefdoctor.org

2. Gap Year for Grown Ups

GapYearForGrownUps offers a number of different ways to help in Africa. Volunteers can pick one out of 12 countries that they work with and do a variety of things. This is a great option for people who are working professionals, or who have perhaps finished studying and want to make a difference. There are different programs from a few days to a few months that will get you involved in animal and wildlife conservation and research and teaching.
inspiredbreaks.co.uk

3. Jinja, Uganda

This small and remote village in Uganda is making a name for itself in being the host to the pioneering company Carpe Diem Education that is responsible for integrating volunteer work and vacation as well as some educational studies at the same time. Helping to develop sustainable gardens, assisting the building of schools, working on farms as well as a bit of exploring the national parks and learning the language, are some of the things volunteers can expect to do here.
carpediemeducation.org

4. Cross-Cultural Solutions

Cross Cultural Solutions is an educational and volunteer program that offers ways to volunteer in Africa for mature people rather than students. Based in Tanzania, the programs run for anything from one whole week right up to 3 entire months. Volunteers are integrated into communities, working side by side with community leaders to bring about change, education and to relieve poverty.
crossculturalsolutions.org

5. The Rhythm of West Africa Semester

A 5-week volunteer initiative that is aimed at students as a combination volunteer and travel package is based in the town of Thies in Senegal, which is just an hour’s drive from Dakar, the capital city. During the 5 weeks, students will attend various seminars, get in touch with the local NGOs, learn to speak the local language that is French, explore the countryside and completely immerse themselves in the remote rural communities that live there, helping out with community projects.
wheretherebedragons.com

6. The Earthwatch Institute

The Earth Watch Institute is currently running some 140 projects in more than 48 different countries – many of them in Africa. Almost half of all of their dedicated volunteers that are working together to make a difference in Africa are mature adults. Their volunteer group attracts social science and scientists from all over the world to help plan systems on sustainability throughout Africa and these are terrific ways of volunteering in Africa for an established professional in any of these fields.
earthwatch.org

7. Doane College

For those intrepid students who want to study but are also looking for ways to make a difference in Africa, this one is for you. Doane Collage offers students the chance to explore wildest Africa, while taking their studies and gaining valuable life experience as well. Participating countries include South Africa, Tanzania, Kenya, Swaziland, Lesotho, Ethiopia, Uganda, Rwanda and Malawi.
doane.edu

8. The Global Volunteer Network

Volunteers will be integrated into a fantastic organic farming project based in Uganda where they will help locals, working together building gardens, digging, planting and building, fertilizing plants, pruning, harvesting and managing all the aspects of sustainable farming. This is a fantastic way to combine anybody’s love of travel, people and volunteering to make a difference.
globalvolunteernetwork.org

9. SIT Study

SIT Study is also known as Social Pluarlism and Development, which is a project based in Yaounde in Cameroon. As part of the package, volunteers move in with a local family for 5 weeks before moving out to the rural areas in a remote area in the north. Combined with a few visits to the coast and the west of Cameroon, it gives volunteers the opportunity to experience more than 200 different ethnic groups and the vibrant communities while completing their studies at the same time.
sit.edu

10. Africa Impact

Many ways to volunteer in Africa involve conservation and wildlife, so Africa Impact is bound to have a program for you if that is your particular area of interest. With everything from primate and wild life conservation in Zambia, eco conservation and nature studies in Swaziland and the rescue and rehabilitation of lions in Zimbabwe, you can really make a difference to the eco-systems essential to our planet.
africanimpact.com

There really are thousands of ways of volunteering in Africa. No matter your age, skills and experience, if you want, you can do your bit. Have you thought about volunteering?

Frequently Asked Questions

What are some safe volunteer opportunities for women in Africa?
Many programs focus on education, healthcare, and wildlife conservation with strong local support to ensure safety.
Do I need prior experience to volunteer in Africa?
While some projects require skills, many welcome passionate beginners, especially in teaching and community work.
How long do volunteer trips usually last?
Trips vary from a week to several months, so you can choose based on your availability and project needs.
What vaccinations or health precautions should I take?
Consult a travel doctor about vaccines like yellow fever and malaria prevention relevant to your destination.
Can I volunteer if I don't speak any African languages?
Many projects use English as a common language, but learning a few local phrases can enrich your experience.
Are there volunteer opportunities that support women’s empowerment?
Yes! Many organizations focus on education, entrepreneurship, and health programs specifically for women.
How can I prepare financially for volunteering in Africa?
Budget for program fees, travel, insurance, and daily expenses, and look for scholarships or fundraising options.
Is it possible to volunteer with children or families?
Yes, some programs welcome families and offer child-friendly activities and accommodations.
How do I choose a reputable volunteer organization?
Research reviews, verify their community impact, and ensure transparency about fees and support.
Can I combine volunteering with tourism in Africa?
Absolutely! Many volunteers explore local culture and wildlife during their free time while contributing to meaningful projects.
