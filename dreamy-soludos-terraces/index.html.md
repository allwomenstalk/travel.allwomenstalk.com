# Terrace Adventures: What I Found with Soludos That Made Me...

Imagine a lazy Sunday afternoon, the gentle rustle of leaves and birdsong in the air, while sipping your favorite drink on a sun-drenched terrace. These are the moments Soludos, known as the _home of the authentic espadrille since 2010_, strives to embody-a blend of relaxation, sunshine, and stories woven into every fabric of our spaces. If you're not already following them, take a peek at their [Instagram](https://www.instagram.com/soludos/) for a glimpse into paradise. Each terrace you’re about to discover shares a narrative, each corner sculpted with a purpose, every seat inviting you to bask in its history and charm.

As a 25-year-old woman constantly on the lookout for inspiration, these scenic spots drew me in with their whispered stories of past and present. From the sprawling grandeur of Colonial Revival estates to minimalist Mykonos getaways, each space sings its own tune. Let me take you on a tour of these enchanting terraces that merge tradition with modernity, each offering its unique flavor of serenity and style.

## The Colonial Revival Charm at Toad Hall

The Toad Hall estate, a resplendent example of Colonial Revival architecture nestled in Aiken, South Carolina, boasts an elegance that’s the epitome of southern grace. Architect Willis Irvin, with his deft touch, crafted a house in 1929 that still resonates with warmth and grandeur after redesign by Mario Buatta.

Picture lounging on this porch strewn with soft, pastel-hued cushions, the aroma of magnolias wafting through the air. The plush chairs invite hours of conversation and contemplation, surrounded by nature’s lush embrace. Each piece of furniture, from the delicate swing to the white wicker sofas, feels as if it’s a silent guardian of countless tales.

**Imagine yourself here-what story would you write against this green and tranquil backdrop?**

## Rebecca Körner's Mykonian Masterpiece

Next, we journey to the sunlit shores of Mykonos, Greece, where interior designer Rebecca Körner has deftly combined mid-century modern pieces with natural materials. The terrace here is nothing short of a masterpiece.

![image](https://img.allw.mn/uploads/91706b4a-d92d-4bf0-98ad-cb092353b8cd_1440x1440.jpg)

Photo: @soludos

Under the warm Mediterranean sun, this terrace captures a laid-back charm while showcasing stunning views of the azure sea. The rustic wooden chairs and bold chandelier anchored by a minimalist table create an effortlessly chic look.

Here, conversations flow as smoothly as a refreshing breeze passing through, with the charm of unrefined wood mingling with the elegant finishings. Each element resonates with a harmony that seems to be custom-tailored for lazy afternoons turned evenings under the stars.

**What would you pair with this setting: a morning espresso or an evening cocktail?**

## Aldo’s Terrace at Le Sirenuse Hotel

In Positano, Italy, the Le Sirenuse Hotel is a jewel of the Amalfi coast-and Aldo’s terrace is its crown. With breathtaking views of the serene Mediterranean, this spot might just be the perfect blend of elegance and extravagance.

![image](https://img.allw.mn/uploads/3444b728-afef-4b76-b413-a21c3ff3e5a6_1440x1440.jpg)

Photo: @soludos

Unapologetically romantic, the simplicity of iron chairs highlights the elaborate beauty of the backdrop. The terrace serves as a perfect vantage point to watch the sea glisten under the sun, crafting an image straight out of a dream. Here, conversations are whispered into the breeze with a promise of love and laughter lingering in the air.

**If you could dine here, what's on your dream plate?**

## Oscar de la Renta’s Captivating Haven

The next stop on our journey brings us to the Dominican Republic, where Oscar de la Renta’s home creates a symphony of natural beauty and interior artistry.

![image](https://img.allw.mn/uploads/7fe6ef76-5ee2-44a2-b334-2e1411e3bdec_1440x1440.jpg)

Photo: @soludos

A rich tapestry of patterns frames the cozy outdoor nook, seamlessly blending with the lush greenery surrounding it. Each woven chair, each ceramic piece tells stories of the designer’s affinity with texture and color.

The terrace here feels both nostalgic and modern, a beautiful paradox grounded in tranquility. It’s the perfect blend of luxury and hominess-where the ocean breeze is a constant companion.

**Do you prefer your escapes more on the rustic side or with a touch of luxury?**

## Christian Louboutin’s Portuguese Retreat

Finally, we arrive at Christian Louboutin’s house in Melides, Portugal, with interiors designed by Egyptian architect Tarek Shamma. It’s a delight for those who cherish a fusion of eclectic and sophisticated design.

![image](https://img.allw.mn/uploads/661a4e29-93f9-4783-a5de-1ff97697aa7c_1039x1039.jpg)

Photo: @soludos

In true Portuguese style, the open-air terrace boasts architectural clean lines, punctuated with whimsical details in vibrant, timeless prints. The space is made for laughter with friends, stories around a fire, and basking in a quiet afternoon glow.

Such a space doesn't just invite gathering-it demands it, bringing together both nature and nurture in an elegant embrace that’s pure Louboutin.
