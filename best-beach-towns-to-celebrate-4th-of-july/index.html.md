# 10 Best Beach Towns to Celebrate 4th of July ...

If you’re planning a seaside break for one of the biggest party days of the year, you need to know **the best beach towns to celebrate 4th July**.

It’s nearly that time of year again. The first week of July contains a holiday all Americans love to celebrate with big parties and even bigger fireworks! The 4th of July is always a great excuse to get away for a few days and combine your national celebration with a long weekend, so the only decision that you have to make is where exactly you want to go! Allow me to help you out a little bit and whittle down the options. Here are ten of the **best beach towns to celebrate 4th July**.

## 1. Bristol, Rhode Island

![reflection, bridge, dawn, sky, sunset,](https://resize.allw.mn/1028x0/filters:format(webp)/filters:quality(70)/content/ky/dw/bmd0ctgv5b202980cb22a075117734_600x750.jpg)

Bristol hosts the nation’s oldest Fourth of July celebrations, which pretty much makes it the number one destination for anybody who is looking to celebrate in true authentic style! It doesn’t hurt that the destination is also quaint and beautiful!

## 2. Long Beach, California

![photograph, vacation, girl, fun, honeymoon,](https://resize.allw.mn/1028x0/filters:format(webp)/filters:quality(70)/content/ae/an/rppztrm35b20299aea50e573332715_600x750.jpg)

If you have kids, then why not take them to Long Beach so they can compete in the annual Great American 4th of July Kids Bike Parade! It’s a red, white and blue bonanza that is unique to the California location, one of the **best beach towns for 4th July celebrations**.

## 3. Southport, North Carolina

![sky, stemware, shore, sea, water,](https://resize.allw.mn/1028x0/filters:format(webp)/filters:quality(70)/content/k9/s7/d2404qjw5b2029bde6c16151149112_600x600.jpg)

This place as named the Happiest Seaside Town in 2015, and it still possesses that positive nature to this day. It hosts two parades, a full day of beach events and a sailing regatta, plus many more delights that you will have to see for yourself!

## 4. Traverse City, Michigan

![girl, tree, water, leg, path,](https://resize.allw.mn/1028x0/filters:format(webp)/filters:quality(70)/content/uo/gp/rqljyupx5b2029ddb311f767564204_600x750.jpg)

One of the Great Lakes beach towns, Traverse City has Fourth of July coincide with their National Cherry Festival, so you get to enjoy Independence Day with a breakfast of irresistible cherry pancakes!

## 5. St. Augustine, Florida

![property, leisure, resort, water feature, hacienda,](https://resize.allw.mn/1028x0/filters:format(webp)/filters:quality(70)/content/d4/k4/vqpnsvxm5b2029fdef680172626323_600x738.jpg)

St. Augustine obviously has amazing weather going for it, but you will also be able to enjoy an amazing fireworks display that goes on for twenty minutes and features more than 5000 exploding shells!

## 6. Murrells Inlet, South Carolina

![sky, horizon, sea, pier, cloud,](https://resize.allw.mn/1028x0/filters:format(webp)/filters:quality(70)/content/pa/cn/m9uhx09s5b202a4964d70338366457_433x650.jpg)

This is something of an undiscovered gem just a mere ten miles up the road from the more famous Myrtle Beach. Everything here is pretty much all focused on the glorious water, including a boat parade and a fireworks show based out of the marshwalk.

## 7. Huntington Beach, California

![clothing, shoulder, girl, vacation, leg,](https://resize.allw.mn/1028x0/filters:format(webp)/filters:quality(70)/content/zo/ps/agm280sl5b202a6d7670f818604848_600x750.jpg)

Know more commonly as Surf City USA, this is the perfect beach town to visit, not just on Fourth of July! If you’re looking for sand, surf and a whole lot of partying, then this should be your first choice for sure.

## 8. Waveland, Mississippi

![sky, landmark, cloud, arch, fixed link,](https://resize.allw.mn/1028x0/filters:format(webp)/filters:quality(70)/content/m7/kk/by6ecznb5b202aab6c3d8658511994_600x600.jpg)

This is a little Gulf town that once won a $50,000 dollar all expenses paid for Fourth of July party experience! You can enjoy BBQ, music, entertainment and of course, fireworks. It’s a town with an all-American spirit!

## 9. San Juan Island, Washington

![water, loch, mammal, waterway, river,](https://resize.allw.mn/1028x0/filters:format(webp)/filters:quality(70)/content/ns/i1/nmh90qbe5b202ac5bb5ca967192679_600x600.jpg)

This is probably the only place in the country where you can celebrate Independence Day at the same time as attending an historic Pig War picnic! The town is old-fashioned, quaint and friendly, and makes for the perfect short getaway.

## 10. Catalina Island, California

![yellow, beauty, vacation, girl, fashion model,](https://resize.allw.mn/1028x0/filters:format(webp)/filters:quality(70)/content/vv/y0/h4po47qo5b202bb1adee1958964761_600x546.jpg)

Catalina Island hosts a brilliant golf cart parade every year that just adds to the old-fashioned and quaint vibe of the place. You can enjoy fireworks over Avalon Bay and the musical delights of the USC Marching Band!
